generate smoke. Obviously, something must have gone wrong. Otherwise how could a Government register a federation and a few days later deregister it along with the other federations? Suffice it to say, Sir, that this was not unconnected with the issue of an entry visa for a Communist Russian representative to come to Singapore. From time to time, the Singapore Government is queried by the Internal Security Council for not taking the advice of the Singapore Government's own Intelligence section. The Intelligence section had recommended against the issue of a visa for a Russian communist representative to come to Singapore for trade. However, the Minister for Finance, together with the Minister for Home Affairs, had over-ruled this objection and had allowed the visa. Meanwhile, the same Intelligence department of the Singapore Government had given advice that a certain general employees' union should not be allowed to register with a federation unless their rues were changed to comply with the new Trade Unions Ordinance and they had first divested themselves of those sections of their membership not qualified to be members of the union under the new trade union laws. This advice was endorsed by the Minister for Home Affairs but, unfortunately, it was overlooked by the Minister for Labour and Law, for he had allowed a federation which included this particular general employees' union to be registered on the' promise of this union that when the new laws came into effect they would comply with them. The question that had been raised in the Internal Security Council was over the visa for a Russian representative on the principle that the Singapore Government had on an important matter ignored advice from the Singapore Government's own Intelligence department. The view of the Singapore Government was that it had the right to over-rule advice from its Intelligence section where it deemed it was in the public interest, and where it considered no grave security risk was involved. But in the case of the advice on the registration of the federation, this advice from the Singapore Government's

Column: 565

It was decided to deregister all the federations and allow them to re-register after the new trade union laws had come into force. The new trade union laws would make it incumbent on general unions to comply first with the 19 categories laid down in the Schedule before they can register as federations.
