    Dr Goh Keng Swee: Mr Speaker, Sir, a secret ballot of employees is usually taken where an employer refuses to accept an invitation to negotiate on grounds that he does not recognise the union. Where there are two or more unions claiming representation for the same group of employees and the matter cannot be resolved in any other way, a secret ballot can then be taken with the consent of the unions concerned. No question of recognition therefore arises. A secret ballot on which union is to represent which grade of employees does not, therefore, arise.

WATER SITUATION
(Statement by the Prime Minister)


    The Prime Minister (Mr Lee Kuan Yew): Sir, I want to report to the House on the water situation.

    Heavy rainfall over the whole Island in the past 10 days, including 4.32 inches for Tuesday, the 16th of January, alone, has brought water stock in our Singapore Reservoirs to full capacity, Rainfall for the month of January up to yesterday, 24th January, has been 14.95 inches as against the January average of 9 inches for the last 86 years. This has enabled the Water Department temporarily to lift rationing and to supply our total consumer needs solely from our Singapore Reservoirs. This will also take advantage of the Singapore rains while conserving stocks in our Johore Reservoirs which are still low because there were no correspondingly heavy rains there in the last fortnight.

    However, the general situation of our total stocks is not altogether satisfactory. About two-thirds of our water stocks are in our Johore Reservoirs of Pontian and Gunong Pulai and these are only 53 per cent of the full capacity, making a 47 per cent shortage. However, in expectation of further rainfall for the rest of January and normal rainfall for February rationing is being lifted.

    The Water Department does not expect water rationing to become necessary again unless a drought were to recur later in the year. Everyone can help by checking waste either in homes or at public standpipes.

    Sir, the summary of the figures for the Johore reservoirs, which I propose to table, reads as follows:

    Gunong Pulai, total capacity, 1,220 million gallons: shortage, 550 million gallons.

    Pontian, maximum capacity, 2,525 million gallons: shortage, 1,218 million gallons.
