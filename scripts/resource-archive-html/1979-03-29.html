(6) Home and Community Environment

    The Study Team's Report has essentially confined itself to the formal aspects of the education system - structure, administration, curriculum, streaming, teacher assessment, etc. We agree that these issues must be given priority attention. At the same time, it is important for us to recognise that the ability of the child to learn is not determined solely by the efficacy of the teaching techniques, learning processes in the school, or the school administration. There are other factors which affect the child's predisposition to be (or not to be) attentive, keen, interested or willing to pursue his studies with joy and deep commitment. Most of these factors arise from problems at home and the child's environment outside school. If we really want to help the child to develop, it is also important for us to minimise the influence that these adverse factors at home and the child's neighbourhood have on the child.

    We must give this subject more serious consideration, not just lip service. We must strive to understand the child, his environment, and all key factors which affect his educational performance. And we must consider this endeavour as an integral part of our education system.

The successful child; his mind and life pattern

    To me, a successful child is one who has the disposition or habit or discipline to want to study. He gets personal satisfaction in both the process or studying, and the impact of understanding and acquiring knowledge. Studying to him, therefore, becomes a purposeful effort and offers him a means to seek and experience self-worth and self-esteem.

    Such a state of mind or character gives the child a kind of momentum or inclination to pursue his studies as if it is part of his natural self. He becomes keen, serious and enthusiastic about his studies and he is faithful and conscientious about his daily

Criticality of the child's environment

In that review, it is stated that research has shown that the brain grows in two stages. First, between the 10th and 1 8th week of pregnancy, the adult number of nerve cells develops.
