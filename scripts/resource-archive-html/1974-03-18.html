SINGAPORE BUS SERVICE LIMITED
(Unroadworthy buses)


    3. Encik Abdul Aziz Karim asked the Minister for Communications how many buses of the Singapore Bus Service (Private) Limited were certified as not roadworthy and their use considered dangerous to the general public, the passengers, the drivers and the conductors.

    Mr Yong Nyuk Lin: Mr Speaker, Sir, all public service vehicles, including buses, are required to undergo inspection by the Registry of Vehicles (ROV) once every six months. They are summoned for such inspection according to a schedule drawn up by the ROV. At these half-yearly inspections, the following are the main items checked: -

    (a) Steering;

    (b) Brakes;

    (c) Lighting;

    (d) Tyres;

    (e) Exhaust;

    (f) Chassis, Body and Suspension

    (g) Engine and Transmission

    (h) Accessories - e.g, rear view mirror, windscreen wiper, horn, speedometer;

    (i) General - e.g, impending failure of the vehicle or any of its equipment or fittings, condition of seating, paint work, bodywork and general condition.

    Any bus which does not meet the minimum standards at these inspections is required to have the defects rectified and be presented again for re-inspection. There have been instances of buses having to be presented several times for re-inspection before being certified fit for use on the road. Sometimes, buses with serious defects are ordered to be scrapped.

    Since the Singapore Bus Service Ltd, began operations in November last year and is a carry-over of operations from the three bus companies, a total of 1,047 of its buses were scheduled by the ROV for the half-yearly inspections

Column: 249

up to the end of last month, February. Of these, 778 buses passed the inspections and were certified fit. Of the other 269, about 215 did not appear for inspection. This sometimes happens when a bus is undergoing repair or overhaul. In such cases, the ROV usually summons the bus for inspection again after one month. About 7 per cent of these buses which were summoned and appeared for inspection did not pass for a variety of defects. The owners were required to have the defects rectified and to present them again for reinspection. None of SBS's buses has, since 1st November last year, been found by the ROV at the inspections to be so unroadworthy that they had to be scrapped.

    It should be stated, however, that 107 buses out of a total registered fleet of 2,081 under the name of SBS are officially laid-up, i.e, these 107 buses or 5.1 per cent are taken off the roads because they are either unroadworthy due to being badly damaged beyond repair or have exceeded the permissible age limit for public buses.     Mr Yong Nyuk Lin: Mr Speaker, Sir, there should be no delay on the part of the Registry of Vehicles in licensing goods vehicles, if the proper document, i.e. the weight certificate of the goods vehicle, is available to determine its maximum laden weight for purpose of registration. Unfortunately, such proper documents are not available for a large number of imported used or re-conditioned goods vehicles. For such cases, the ROV has requested the local agent to check these vehicles against their manufacturers' specifications and to issue a certificate which is acceptable for registration.     Since March, 1973, the agent has issued 987 such certificates for goods vehicles inspected by them. In addition, ROV has, since August, 1973, accepted 152 goods vehicles for registration based on the submission of an extract of registration particulars or plating certificate from the country of origin. The latter measure was undertaken to expedite the registration process. As at 9th March, 1974, the number of vehicles on the waiting list for issue of maximum laden weight certificate by the agent is 227. It has been estimated that the agent could issue up to 20 certificates a week.

    It should be stated that on 28th March last year, the Registrar of Vehicles did inform representatives of leading importers of used goods vehicles of the need for proper documents to support the registration and licensing of such
