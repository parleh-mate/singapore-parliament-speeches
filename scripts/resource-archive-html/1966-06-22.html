PESIARAN KELILING
(Traffic Arrangements)


    1. Mr S. V. Lingam asked the Deputy Prime Minister whether he will give early consideration to (a) making Pesiaran Keliling a one-way street; (b) permitting the extension of bus services into the Macpherson Housing Estate; and (c) the parking of vehicles on one side only of Pesiaran Keliling.

    The Minister of State for the Prime Minister's Department (Tuan Haji Yaacob Bin Mohamed) (for the Deputy Prime Minister)(In Malay): Mr Speaker, Sir, in answer to (a) , entry into Macpherson Housing Estate and Pesiaran Keliling is via Jalan Merpati and exit via Mattar Road. Both these approach and exit roads are made into one-way streets because of their narrowness. Mattar Road is 18 feet wide and Jalan Merpati 22 feet wide.

    Pesiaran Keliling, however, is 31 feet wide and the total distance is 1.1 miles round the Estate from its commencement at its junction with Jalan Merpati. This road was constructed sufficiently wide enough to carry two-way traffic. If this road were to be converted into one-way, it will be causing undue hardship and unnecessary inconvenience to the residents in this Housing Estate.

    In answer to (b) , the Omnibus Services Licensing Authority has approved extensions of bus services into the Macpherson Housing Estate. The Omni-bus Services Licensing Authority has approved two S.T.C. bus services which would operate in this Housing Estate. The first is a deviation of part of S.T.C. Service No. 12, to be known as Service No. 12A, from Macpherson Housing Estate via Jalan Besar, New Bridge Road/North Bridge Road to Shenton Way. The second is an extension of S.T.C. Service No. 9 from Shenton Way via Alexandra Road, Tanglin Road, Orchard Road and Serangoon Road to Macpherson Estate.

    The Omnibus Services Licensing Authority also has before it two applications from the Tay Koh Yat Bus Co. Ltd. to deviate its Service No. 11 from the junction of Aljunied Road/Geylang

Column: 104

Road to the junction of Upper Serangoon Road/Woodsville Circus, and Service No. 12 from the junction of Aljunied Road/Geylang Road to the junction of Macpherson Road/Woodsville Circus into Macpherson Estate. These two applications were advertised in the local Press on 13th May, 1966 and will be considered by the Omnibus Services Licensing Authority one month after their publication.

    In answer to (c) , it is proposed to draw a continuous white line along Pesiaran Keliling, Jalan Pipit and Jalan Balam. This will ensure that no vehicles are parked along this road and there would be a smooth flow of traffic. Sufficient car parks and parking bays have been provided in this housing estate to cater for the residents. The car parks and parking bays are now occupied by unlicensed hawkers, causing the owners of motor cars to park their vehicles on the road proper. This has created some confusion along Pesiaran Keliling, particularly at its junction with Jalan Merpati. Once the hawkers are removed to the market, which has been completed recently, the car parks and parking bays could be used for the purpose for which they are constructed. It will not, therefore, be necessary for residents to park their vehicles on the road proper.

    5. Mr P. Govindaswamy asked the Minister for Health (a) how many beds there are in each hospital, giving separate figures for beds in the different classes, where applicable; (b) whether the number of beds in each type of hospital is adequate to meet present needs; (c) whether there is a programme for expansion of hospital bed accommodation to meet future needs, and if he will give details; and (d) if he will consider setting up an emergency unit at the Thomson Road Hospital for the treatment of casualties.

    Mr Yong Nyuk Lin: Mr Speaker, Sir, in reply to the first part of question (a) , there are 14 Government hospitals in Singapore with a total of 6,797 staffed beds at present, of which the majority 6,433 or 90 per cent are free of charge (i.e. 'C' Class). There are 249 'B' Class beds at a charge of $12 per day and 115 'A' Class beds at a charge of $30 per day or $40 per day, if air-conditioned.

    As the hon. Member has asked for details of the number of beds in each hospital, and their classification as well, I am therefore submitting the details in a separate schedule for tabling and circulation instead.

    With regard to (b) , as a general rule, it can reasonably be said that the present bed capacity in Government hospitals as a whole are adequate, but the following three are either congested or under great pressure, namely, Woodbridge Hospital, Outram Road General Hospital and Kandang Kerbau Hospital.

    Before answering (c) of the question on proposed programme of expansion, may I, Sir, indicate certain steps which have already been taken to lessen the

Column: 108

present congestion or pressures of the three named hospitals?

    Firstly, in the case of Woodbridge Hospital, thanks to the efficacy of modern drugs and in accordance with modern practice, it has been possible for more and more patients to be discharged from hospital and to continue treatment in what are known as Day Centres. Secondly, as Trafalgar Home, which adjoins Woodbridge Hospital, is only half-full, a section of Trafalgar Home is now being renovated and converted for the transfer of the chronically sick from Woodbridge Hospital, in order to relieve the congestion at the latter hospital.

    In the case of Outram Road General Hospital, it has been necessary to alleviate the pressure of numbers by a quicker turnover of patients and the average length of stay of patients is around nine days per patient. It is agreed that this is not an ideal method, but has to be resorted to until such time as more hospitals can be built and, what is even more important, more staff, i.e, doctors, nurses and technicians, and equipment are available.

    An effort, however, has already been made to relieve the pressures on Outram Road General Hospital with the setting up of two surgical theatres in Thomson Road Hospital in January 1965, thus converting it into Singapore's second General Hospital.

    Two effective steps have already been taken to relieve the pressure in Kandang Kerbau Hospital. In order to encourage greater use of our Free Domiciliary Midwifery service, a nominal charge of $10 was levied for 'C' Class patients from December 1964. Next, Government has given full publicity for some time regarding the need to offer Singapore mothers the latest techniques in Family Planning and have, in fact, established a Family Planning and Population Board in January this year to implement a 5-Year Family Planning programme in all Maternal and Child Health Centres.

    As for the response to Family Planning under the auspices of the Family Planning and Population Board, I am pleased to say that for the first 5 months of this year, a total of 15,718 mothers are now receiving family planning assistance, made up of 4,691 new cases and the balance being former Singapore Family Planning Association cases.

ALSAGOFF ESTATE
(Allocation of house numbers)


    6. Inche Mohd. Ariff Bin Suradi asked the Minister for Finance whether the Government will give house numbers to the houses built before 1st April, 1960, in Alsagoff Estate whose house owners are paying ground rent to the landlords or their agents.

    The Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for Finance (Mr S. Ramaswamy) (for the Minister for Finance): Mr Speaker, Sir, when applications for house numbers are made to the Comptroller of Property Tax, who has the authority for the issue of house numbers in the Republic, house numbers will be issued, where the applicants produce either a temporary building permit or a letter of no objection from the Chief Building Surveyor. House numbers will not generally be issued except where either of these is furnished, as it is the practice of the Inland Revenue Department not to allocate house numbers to unauthorised buildings. Accordingly, the owners of the houses mentioned in the question by the hon. Member should apply to the Chief Building Surveyor for either a temporary building permit or a letter of no objection; and after obtaining same, forward them together with their applications for house numbers to the Comptroller of Property Tax, Inland Revenue Department, Property Tax Division, Government Offices, City Hall, St. Andrew's Road, Singapore. The Comptroller will then take the necessary action to number the houses.

    As regards the matter of ground rent, it can be mentioned that the Chief Building Surveyor may require production of ground rent receipts by applicants for temporary building permits or letters of no objection in order to establish the identity of the owners of the structures concerned.
