    Mr Speaker: On the 23rd day of December, 1960, the then hon.     The article in question was captioned. "Ong must cite law to back his motion". The Member referred to two paragraphs therein and invited my opinion as to whether - to quote his words - "any of the passages referred to is a matter which is sub judice".

    I then expressed the opinion that there was no matter which could be considered sub judice at that moment, the expression "sub judice" being used with reference to a court of law. Hon. Members will of course know, for example, that under Standing Order No. 19 relating to the contents of questions, no question may be asked which reflects on the decision of a court of law and no question shall be asked on any matter which is sub judice; and again under Standing Order No. 46 relating to the contents of speeches, reference cannot be made to any matter which is sub judice except by means of or in the course of consideration of a Bill when reference may be made, but only in such a way as will not, in the opinion of the Speaker or Chairman, prejudice the interests of parties thereto. I might add that in the application of those Standing Orders the term "sub judice" has been construed to have reference to matters pending in a court of law and in a Commission of Inquiry.

    The then Member for Hong Lim, however, in response to my enquiry, stated that he wished to raise his point as a matter of privilege, drawing my attention to the fact that the report was contained not only in the Straits Times but also in other newspapers. I do not think that the fact that the same report or article is contained in more than one newspaper could make any difference to a ruling as to whether there has been any breach of privilege.

    The passages quoted by the then Member for Hong Lim as being in breach of privilege of this Assembly are-

    Firstly:

    "This right has been admitted in the case of the House of Commons and in all Legislatures patterned after it."

The right referred to has reference to the right mentioned in the article in the two

    Secondly, that is to say, the second passage quoted by the then Member for Hong Lim is as follows:-

    In regard to the first passage, it is apparent that it merely purported to be an analysis of the legal position which in fact had been urged by the Deputy Prime Minister in his speech delivered when he moved the motion, as is apparent in the next passage in the article in question which reads as follows:-

    "Dr Toh, in his speech on Monday, had cited cases to show that the Singapore Assembly had these rights too."

    In regard to the second passage, it appears to be a paraphrase of what I myself said, albeit not exactly a complete or accurate paraphrase. The passage has been taken out of context and must be considered in the light of the following paragraph which reads:-

    "However, he expressed doubts as to what extent the extreme powers of expulsion and suspension could be exercised by the Assembly."

    If the then Member for Hong Lim in drawing my attention to the passages quoted by him and by using the term "sub judice" sought to infer that the passages are in the nature of comment calculated to prejudice the proceedings pending on the motion of the Deputy Prime Minister, then I am bound to say that I disagree; I take the view that there is nothing in those passages, more especially when not read out of context, which can be said to have that effect.

PROPERTY TAX BILL


    Order for Second Reading read.

2.48 p.m.

    The Minister for Finance (Dr Goh Keng Swee): Mr Speaker, I have the Yang di-Pertuan Negara's recommendation that this Bill be proceeded with. Sir, I beg to move, "That the Bill be read a Second time."

    Sir, the object of this Bill is to provide for the levy of a tax on immovable properties in lieu of rates leviable under the Municipal Ordinance and the Local Government Ordinance, 1957, as on integration of the City Council into the Central Government, it will be no longer proper to levy Local Government rates on lands and buildings, since these are made on the basis of services and utilities of equivalent value being provided from the revenue so obtained. It will be necessary, therefore, to introduce a new system of taxation to replace these rates.

    The present Bill follows closely the procedure laid down in the Local Government Ordinance for the assessment of annual value of property and collection of rates.

    At present, rates are collected for the City area under powers contained in the Local Government Ordinance and for the rural areas under the Municipal Ordinance. The consolidated rate payable in respect of properties in the City area is now 36 per cent and section 7 of the Property Tax Ordinance lays down that the property tax payable each year shall be at this same rate of 36 per cent.

    Some areas outside the City limits are, however, paying rates at less than 36 per cent applicable to the City area. It is intended to continue this practice of charging lower rates for rural areas and the first proviso to section 7 enables the Minister to direct that the rate or tax in such areas shall be such rate or rate less than 36 per cent.

    The property tax proposals for 1961 are briefly as follows.

    At present, properties within City limits are assessed at 36 per cent and properties in the rural areas are assessed at 14 per cent, 18 per cent, 20 per cent, 23 per cent, or 36 per cent consolidated

rates. Instead of this multiplicity of rates, there will be only three rates of Property Tax for occupied lands, which will be 36 per cent, 27 per cent and 18 per cent.

    Properties at present paying 36 per cent rates will continue to pay 36 per cent Property Tax on the annual value ascribed to the property. There are certain areas which previously were outside the City limits but which have been brought into the City area by the Local Government Ordinance, 1957, as defined in the First Schedule to the Ordinance. They comprise largely the areas at Pasir Panjang, Buona Vista, Ulu Pandan, Holland Road and Ulu Kallang. These areas were assessed at 23 per cent when they were in the rural areas and the assessment of 23 per cent was continued, although they were brought into the City limits with the coming into force of the Local Government Ordinance. These areas will now pay the rate of 36 per cent instead of 23 per cent.

    There are also a number of housing estates in the rural areas which pay 23 per cent assessment. They are Adelphi Park, Sembawang Hill, Thomson Rise, Windsor Park, Serangoon Garden, Seletar Hill, Opera Estate, Spring and Bedok Estates, Hongkong Park, Bukit Timah Park and King Albert Park. These housing estates have enjoyed urban facilities as lighting, water, roads and so forth, and will therefore pay 36 per cent rate.

    (i) ½ mile on either side of Bukit Timah Road from approximately 8 ½ m.s. and along Woodlands Road up to the Causeway;

    (iii) areas at Serangoon, Paya Lebar, Bedok, Changi and Loyang.

    (i) the present undeveloped western portions of the Island, i.e., Jurong;

    (ii) the undeveloped northern and north-eastern parts comprising Bukit Panjang, Sembawang, Serangoon and Katong;

    (iii) some of the surrounding islands, i.e., Pulau Ubin, Pulau Tekong Kechil, Pulau Tekong Besar, Pulau Blakang Mati, Pulau Bukom, Pulau Bukom Kechil and a few other islands.

    The above rates will be payable on houses or occupied lands. The rate for vacant lands is at present 36 per cent for properties in the City area and this 36 per cent for vacant lands will apply to the 36 per cent areas for 1961.

    The rate for vacant lands in the 27 per cent and 18 per cent areas will be at 18 per cent and 12 per cent respectively.

    Details of the proposals will be gazetted shortly and a plan will be made available for inspection showing the rates applicable to the whole Island.

    These proposals are not final and the public will be given an opportunity up to 31st March, 1961, to make representations regarding the inclusion of any particular area or locality in one of the three rates of tax proposed.

    I have stated in my Budget Statement that the present practice of assessing owner-occupied properties on favourable terms is irregular and that it is intended to stop this practice. However, in order not to penalise owner-occupiers of small properties, the rates of taxation will be fixed at less than the general rate. It will obviously take some time for the owner-occupied houses to be re-valued at the proper values. Until the re-valuation of these properties has been completed, owner-occupiers will continue to pay property rates at the proposed rates on present values. Similarly, provision is also made in respect of industrial premises in order to ensure that industrial undertakings are not hampered through the operation of the Bill.

However, provision is made for exemption of deserving cases of this nature and of buildings used exclusively for purposes conducive to social development. It will also be mandatory for him to notify the Chief Assessor when a building ceases to be occupied by the owner thereof and is let. Because the tax is a tax on the ownership of property irrespective of whether it is occupied or not, refunds of tax for "letted" buildings which have become unoccupied during the tax year will cease.

    Special provisions have been made for assessment of taxes on new buildings, buildings which have been rebuilt or enlarged, properties assessed for the first time, and properties which should have been assessed for rates under the Municipal Ordinance or the Local Government Ordinance prior to the coming into operation of this Ordinance but have not been so assessed. It is provided that in all these cases the tax shall be payable from the date of completion of the building or in the case of land from such date, not being more than six years prior to the date on which the property is first assessed, as may be determined by the Comptroller. In cases where a building ceases to be occupied by the owner and is let or where the rent thereof is increased, the tax payable from the date of such letting or increase of rent is to be calculated on the basis of a revised value which may subsequently be ascribed to such building.
