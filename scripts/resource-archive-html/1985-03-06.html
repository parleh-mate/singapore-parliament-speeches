SOCIAL DEVELOPMENT UNIT
(Particulars)


    1. Mr J.B. Jeyaretnam asked the Minister for Finance, Minister for Education and Minister for Health if he will state how much of the tax-payers' monies has been spent by the Social Development Unit as of the 15th February 1985 and with what results.

     The Minister for Finance, Minister for Education and Minister for Health (Dr Tony Tan Keng Yam): Mr Speaker, Sir, the total amount that has been spent by the Social Development Unit on its activities as at 15th February, 1985, was $294,411.72.

    The Unit's objectives are long-term:

    (i) To create an awareness of the problem of the high incidence of singlehood among our graduate women; and

    (ii) To bring about a change of attitude towards marriage in our younger single officers.

    Some progress has been made towards these objectives. More single officers are participating in the activities organized by the Unit. These officers are prepared to pay the cost of participation which varies from 15% to 100% of full costs depending on whether the activity is a training programme or an organized tour.

    The single officers are also more open in discussing marriage and their choice of partners. Through attending the activities organized by the Social Development Unit, these single officers have come to terms with what they want from life and more are deciding that marriage should be part of their lives.

    Let me explain that in Singapore the price of land is based on two factors, ie, the zoning and the density. In the Singapore Master Plan every piece of land is zoned for a specific use. For example, if a piece of land is zoned "residential", it means that residential development is allowed. If the land is zoned "commercial", it means commercial development will be allowed. If the land is zoned "hotels", it means that hotels will be allowed to develop on the land. If the land is zoned "open spaces", it means that it will remain as open spaces and no development will be allowed other than recreational facilities. The price of the land is based on the zoning.

    Another factor, as I will explain, is density. If a piece of land is zoned "residential" with a density of 100 persons per hectare, the developer may build flats for say, four persons per flat (that is around 1,000 sq ft per flat, or around 100 sq metres.) So on one hectare of land he will be allowed to build 25 flats. If the density is 200 persons per hectare, 50 flats can be built. The same goes for "commercial" zoned land. If a piece of commercial land has a plot ratio of one, it means that, for every sq metre of land, the developer is allowed to build one sq metre of commercial space. If the plot ratio is three, it means that for every sq metre of the land, he is allowed to build three sq metres of commercial space. These are the two factors which determine the value of land: the zoning and the density or plot ratio.
