    We in Singapore live in a tight economic situation. We are a small island State which is prosperous because of our central geographical position. We will continue to earn this livelihood so long as we provide these services efficiently and so long as we are on good terms with our neighbours. Review, of 1961

    The year 1961 was an eventful one. Several new financial and economic measures were successfully introduced. Others initiated by the P.A.P. Government in previous years came to fruition during this period. I wish to deal briefly with some of the more important of these financial and economic measures.

The Development Plan sets out the targets in economic and social development which the State should achieve in the period 1961-1964. The principal objective of the Plan is to increase the rate of capital formation from its present level of about 11 per cent of the national income to something nearer 20 per cent. This is to ensure an expansion of the economy sufficient to keep pace with population growth and to leave aside something for the improvement of standards of living. City Council integration

    Sir, before I comment on the Estimates now before the House, I wish to dwell at some length on the progress made on the integration of the City Council departments into the Government. This is a major administrative reform undertaken by the P.A.P. Government. This year's estimates is the first exercise at integrating the Budgets of the Government and certain City Council departments.     The City Council has hitherto been divided into two main parts, the Rate Fund Departments and the Trading Departments. The Rate Fund Departments, which include sewerage, cleansing and many other departments, were financed from rates paid by ratepayers under the Local Government Ordinance, 1957. The Trading departments are the water, gas and electricity departments, and they derive their revenues from the consumers of water, gas and electricity. The intention is to constitute the Trading departments, with the necessary administrative staff, as a Public Utilities Board. The Rate Fund Departments are to be integrated or merged with the existing Ministries of the Government.
