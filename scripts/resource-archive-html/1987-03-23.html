PULAU SAKENG
(Resettlement plans)


    1. Encik Abbas Abu Amin asked the Minister for Law (a) whether there is any plan to resettle the residents of Pulau Seking, the only inhabited island of the Southern Islands; and (b) if there is no definite plan for resettlement, whether the Land Office will further enhance maintenance works and provide facilities for the islanders who are mostly senior citizens.

     The Second Minister for Law (Prof. S. Jayakumar): Mr Speaker, Sir, since Pulau Sakeng is not affected by immediate development, there is no plan at present to resettle the residents of the island.

    There are 65 families (about 250 persons) living on the island. They pay TOL fees of only $3 per annum for a dwelling house and $12 per annum for a shop site. Maintenance work such as oiling and anti-malarial work is being carried out by the Ministry of the Environment every week. The clearing of daily refuse is being carried out by the householders on the island either by burning or burying. Since the existing maintenance services and amenities provided are satisfactory, there is no plan to provide additional facilities or services.

INFLATION IN 1987


    11. Mr Chew Heng Ching asked the Minister for Trade and Industry if he will give the outlook for inflation in Singapore in 1987.

     The Minister for Trade and Industry (BG Lee Hsien Loong):Mr Deputy Speaker, Sir, Singapore experienced an inflation rate of minus 1.4% in 1986, the first annual decline in a decade. This disinflation was mainly attributed to the lower cost of food, clothing, housing and transport and communications.
