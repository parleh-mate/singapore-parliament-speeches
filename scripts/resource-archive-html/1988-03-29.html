HDB MULTI-TIER FAMILY HOUSING SCHEME
(Cases of abuse)


    2. Mrs Yu-Foo Yee Shoon asked the Minister for National Development how many cases of abuse have been detected under the 3-tier family privilege scheme introduced by the Housing and Development Board and how have they been dealt with.

     Mr Dhanabalan: Sir, the Multi-tier Family Housing Scheme was introduced in May 1982. On allocation, the multi-tier family must stay together in the flat for eight years before the flat can be sold in the open market. Within the eight years, if the parents or grandparents are found to be not staying in the flat without valid reasons, the applicant would have breached a condition under the scheme. The HDB would then recover the flat to prevent abuse of the scheme.

    As at 29th February 1988, of the 12,500 flats allocated under the Multi-tier Family Scheme, the HDB recovered 18 flats or 0.14%. Out of these 18 flats, 17 units were returned voluntarily to the HDB because

the parents and the married children did not wish to continue staying together. In the remaining case, the applicant abused the scheme because he did not report to the HDB that the multi-family structure no longer existed. HDB recovered the flat when the Board found this out subsequently.

"LEASE-A-DENTAL CLINIC" SCHEME
(Progress)


    6. Dr Ho Tat Kin asked the Acting Minister for Health what is the progress made by his Ministry's "Lease-A-Dental Clinic" scheme; and whether this scheme has been able to provide better dental care at a lower cost to the people.

     The Acting Minister for Health (Mr Yeo Cheow Tong): Sir, the Lease-A-Dental Clinic Scheme was introduced in 1984. So far, 11 dental clinics with a total of 25 dental chairs have been leased. The Scheme has met its objectives. It is providing young dental officers with the opportunity to gain commercial experience before they venture into their own private practice. So far, 44 dental officers have participated in the Scheme.

    The dentists operating the leased dental clinics are providing a more personalized service. They spend more time on each patient and also offer a much wider range of dental services such as crowns and bridges. Waiting time has also been reduced.

    As for the cost of treatment, it is not possible for the dentists operating the leased dental clinics to charge the same fees as the subsidized Government dental clinics. However, their fees are generally lower than the fees charged by other private dentists providing a similar high degree of personal service.
