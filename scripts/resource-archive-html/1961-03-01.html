During the debate on the Budget Estimates in December last year, Mr Ong Eng Guan, then Member for Hong Lim, made certain allegations against the Prime Minister and the Minister for Labour and Law. Because Mr Ong Eng Guan did not substantiate those allegations, I on the 19th December, 1960, moved a motion in the following terms:-

    `That this House condemns the Member for Hong Lim for his dishonourable conduct unbecoming of an elected representative of the people in that he repeatedly used his privilege in this Assembly as a cloak for spreading malicious falsehoods to unjustly injure innocent persons both inside and outside this Assembly; that he be suspended from the service of this Assembly until such time as (i) he apologises to this Assembly for his dishonourable conduct; (ii) he unreservedly withdraws his unfounded allegations against the Prime Minister and the Minister for Labour and Law; and (iii) he assures the Assembly that he will not persist in abusing his privilege in this Assembly by uttering malicious falsehoods, unless he be prepared when challenged to repeat outside the Assembly the charges he makes in the Assembly.'

In the debate on this motion, I listed three specific allegations made by him. One allegation concerns the re-employment of Mr M. R. Marcus in the City Council. In col. 786 in Hansard, No. 9, Vol. 14, dated 19th December, 1960, Mr Ong Eng Guan said:

    `I wonder if this is the way to get away from the P.S.C. in order to give a job to this particular officer who happens to be a brother-in-law of a Minister.'

The other two allegations concern the appointment of Mr Kwa Soon Chuan as Deputy Commissioner (Valuation). In col. 786 of the same volume of Hansard of the same number, he said:

    `Mr Speaker, Sir, I do not wish to put it to the Minister that many of these recommendations - which I have read myself - are meant for the purpose of fixing up certain things.'

He also said:

    `In my opinion, there is nothing, as far as I know, to merit the transfer other than the fact that he is the brother-in-law of the Prime Minister.'

These were serious allegations as they implied that these officers had been favoured in the civil service because they happened to be related to the Prime Minister and the Minister for Labour and Law.
