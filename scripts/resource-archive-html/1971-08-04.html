ORAL ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS

WATER-CLOSET CISTERNS
(Replacement with direct-flow flush system)


    1. Mr Ng Yeow Chong asked the Prime Minister if he will ask the Public Utilities Board to consider the feasibility of replacing the present water-closet cisterns with a direct-flow flush system to economise water.

    The Minister for Education (Mr Lim Kim San) (for the Prime Minister): Mr Speaker, Sir, I presume the "direct flow flush system" referred to by the

Column: 194

Member is the spring-loaded flush valve system. If so, the Public Utilities Board would not recommend replacing the present water-closet cisterns with spring-loaded flush valves because they are much more expensive, costing about three times as much as the ordinary water-closet cisterns. Besides, they go out of order more easily, resulting in water being wasted as leakage from the valve flows directly into the water closet pan. Such leakage is also difficult to detect. Furthermore, the volume of flush is usually greater and cannot be controlled. Another objectionable feature of the flush valve system is that, in the event of its malfunction, it is

Column: 195

possible for contamination of water due to reverse flow, to take place.

    The Board has, however, in some instances, allowed the installation of flush valve systems in institutional buildings. Very stringent conditions to minimise waste and to prevent the possibility of contamination by the provision of a separate storage tank are laid down as conditions of such approval.

OLD KALLANG AIRPORT ESTATE
(Construction of hawkers' emporium and high-rise flats)


    The following Question stood in the name of Mr Ng Yeow Chong -

    2. To ask the Minister for Law and National Development when work will commence on the construction of a hawkers' emporium and high-rise flats on a site in Old Kallang Airport Estate which was cleared for redevelopment in early 1970, and whether the flats will include 5-room units.

    Mr Hwang Soo Jin (Jalan Kayu) rose -

    Mr Speaker: Mr Hwang Soo Jin.

    Mr Hwang Soo Jin: Sir, I have been asked by the Member for Mountbatten to raise Question No. 2.

    The Minister for Law and National Development (Mr E. W. Barker): Mr Speaker, Sir, the Board proposed to build a 2-storey hawkers' emporium together with 16-storey flats above on a site in the Old Kallang Airport Estate. It has now been decided to abandon the construction of high-rise flats and build only the 2-storey hawkers' emporium. Investigations have revealed that the soil condition in the area is extremely poor, and extensive piling will be required to support a high-rise building. The Board will be calling for tenders at the end of August to construct the proposed 2-storey hawkers' emporium. The project is expected to be completed by early 1972.

    Mr Hwang Soo Jin: Mr Speaker, Sir, two supplementary questions. First,

Column: 196

what will be the capacity of the proposed hawkers' emporium? Second, can the Minister give an indication of the estimated cost of piling per unit if the high-rise flats were to be constructed?

    Mr Barker: Sir, the emporium will comprise 136 lock-up shops on the first floor and 176 hawker stalls on the ground floor.

    As for the second question, the soil investigations have revealed that the length of piling for high-rise flats will have to be more than 150 feet deep. In the circumstances, the piling cost for each 4-room flat in this area will be about $6,000 as against the average cost of about $1,500 for piling in other areas.
