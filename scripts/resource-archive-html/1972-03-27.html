ORAL ANSWER TO QUESTION
ADULT EDUCATION TV PRODUCTION AND TRAINING
CENTRE PROJECT (CEPTA TV)
(Functions and financial commitments)


    1. Mr J. F. Conceicao asked the Minister for Culture what percentage of the total expenses of the Adult Education TV Production and Training Centre Project does his Ministry's share represent; what kind of programmes will be produced, for what audience and where; and what kind of personnel will be trained.

Column: 1222



    The Minister for Culture (Mr Jek Yeun Thong): Mr Speaker, Sir, it is not possible at this initial stage to say precisely what percentage of the total expenses of the Adult Education TV Production and Training Centre Project (known as CEPTA TV) my Ministry's share represents. This is because the project will take three to four years before it becomes fully operational.

    However, an idea can be gauged from its financial commitment which is as follows :

    (i) Government will provide suitable building accommodation for CEPTA TV to house offices, equipment, service and other technical areas. The building is estimated to cost in the region of $1 million;

Column: 1223



    (ii) Government will also provide 50 per cent of the annually recurrent operating costs of CEPTA TV, subject to a maximum of $900,000 per year;

    (iii) The Friedrich Ebert Stiftung of the Federal Republic of Germany will supply equipment and supporting facilities estimated at $6.5 million for CEPTA TV's operation;

    (iv) The Stiftung will also pay 50 per cent of the annually recurrent operating costs of CEPTA TV up to $900,000, plus anything in excess of the joint limit of $1.8 million. The amount of $500,000 provided under Head 51, subhead 103, of the 1972-73 Development Estimates is intended to meet the capital expenditure on the first stage of the building construction.

    CEPTA TV's activities will be carried out on a regional basis and the Centre will produce adult education programmes which will provide useful audio-visual support for national institutions in Asia engaged in the promotion of adult education through television and other audio-visual means. The programmes will be produced in Singapore, for use not only in Singapore, but also in other countries in Asia which participate in the project. The audiences, therefore, will be in Singapore and in the Asian countries that participate.

    The personnel who will be trained include television programmers, engineers, and other personnel in the adult education field. The training of personnel in specific areas of television production will be carried Out in conjunction with the Centre's programme production activities. Much of the training will be given on an on-the-job basis.

    First of all, may I give a brief background to this Bill? There are at present five statutory authorities dealing with

matters relating to seafarers and seamen: -

    (1) Seamen's Registry Board

    (2) Seafarers' Welfare Board

    (3) Singapore Mariners' Club

    (4) Singapore Sailors' Institute

    (5) Seamen's Lodging-Houses Licensing Authority.

The Seamen's Registry Board is responsible for the registration, supply and discipline of seamen, while the Seafarers' Welfare Board looks after the welfare of retired, sick and disabled seamen and their dependents and is also responsible for the training of seamen. The Singapore Mariners' Club operates toe seamen's hostel at South Quay, while the Singapore Sailors' Institute until recently operated Connell House. Connell House reverted to Government in August last year, and its clientele are now using the faculties provided by the renovated Singapore Mariners' Club, inc Seamen's Lodging-Houses Licensing Authority is responsible for issuing licences to seamen's lodging houses, but has been practically a moribund body for some time.

    The above five authorities have historical origins. Circumstances have changed considerably over the last decade and it would be more logical to amalgamate the five bodies into one authority to be known as the National Maritime Board. This move will eliminate over-lapping functions and will result in more efficient organisation and administration of seafarers matters, as well as better co-ordination of the various functions presently performed the five different bodies.

    Sir, I will now summarise briefly the main provisions of this Bill. The Board will consist of nine members with representatives from Government. ship-owners and seamen. Such a compact Board will be able to perform its functions more effectively than a large unwieldy board.

    The objects of the Board are to develop and regulate maritime employment and to administer welfare and

Column: 1274

training schemes. The Board will also advise the Minister on matters relating to seafaring and such other related matters which the Minister may refer to the Board for advice.

    For the proper administration of funds, there is established a National Maritime Fund into which all moneys of the Board shall be paid. The moneys in the existing Singapore Mercantile Marine Fund shall be transferred to The National Maritime Fund. The Board may, with the approval of the Minister, Invest any surplus funds in such stocks and securities and other investments as may be approved by him.

    Clause 19 is a re-enactment of the substance of the Seamen's Registry Board Act which requires shipowners to engage seamen through the Seamen's Registry Board system. Clause 20 provides for penalties both for the employer and seamen in the event of breach of the law.

    The Board is empowered, with the approval of the Minister, to make regulations. These powers include the power to prescribe the procedures by which the seamen may be selected for employment. At present, these procedures are spelt out in the Seamen's Registry Board Act itself, but when the National Maritime Board Bill comes into force it is proposed that such procedures will be re-enacted by way of regulations.

    When the National Maritime Board Act comes into operation, the Seamen's Registry Board, Seafarers' Welfare Board, Singapore Mariners' Club, Connell House Committee and the Seamen's Lodging-Houses Licensing Authority shall cease to exist. However, seamen who are already registered under the Seamen's Registry Board Act shall be deemed to be registered under the National Maritime Board Act. The existing staff of all these authorities shall be deemed to be transferred to the service of the National Maritime Board on terms not less favourable than those they enjoyed immediately prior to their transfer.
