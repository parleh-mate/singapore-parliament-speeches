The first convention was the Tokyo Convention on Offences and Certain Other Acts committed on board aircraft signed in 1963.     The Tokyo Convention has been supplemented by a further multilateral convention signed at The Hague on 16th December, 1970. This is The Hague Convention for the Suppression of Unlawful Seizure of Aircraft which came into force on 14th October, 1971. Singapore signed this Convention on 8th September, 1971. The purpose of this Convention is to provide a legal framework which will ensure that hijackers do not go unpunished.
