     Mr Shanmugam:  Mr Speaker, Sir, the Members of Parliament asked about the gang situation, whether there are trends of concern and the measures taken to curb gang violence.
    
     The rioting situation can, broadly, be used as a proxy indicator of gang violence. Between 2005 and 2009, rioting cases fell from 489 cases to 283 cases.  Between January and September 2010, there were 214 rioting cases.  Cases with secret society connotations have also generally decreased.  These figures are in the handouts* given, so that Members themselves can look at them.  The trends therefore are downward as a result of effective Police action over the years.

 

     There has been understandable public concern over recent reported gang violence. Overall crime rates have fallen in the last five years.  The rates for crimes against persons and violent property crimes in particular have also remained low and stable over the past five years.  And as the handout shows, the trend of gang violence is downwards.
