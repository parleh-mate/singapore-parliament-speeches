NOISE POLLUTION
(Legislation)


    1. Mr Ang Nam Piau asked the Prime Minister whether the Government will consider introducing legislation to provide for anti-noise measures.

Column: 334



     The Minister for the Environment (Mr Lim Kim San) (for the Prime Minister): Mr Speaker, Sir, I have been asked by the Prime Minister to reply to this question.

    Mr Speaker, Sir, at present there are existing regulations enabling several Ministries and departments to act on certain aspects of noise pollution. For instance, the Registrar of Vehicles can take action against

Column: 335

the owner of a vehicle which emits excessive noise. The Ministry of Culture limits the noise coming from outdoor performances and from loudspeakers in record shops, and the Ministry of the Environment can require the occupier of a trade and industrial premises which creates excessive noise to abate the noise. Administratively, the Government pursues a policy against noise pollution. Members are probably aware that Radio and Television Singapore, for instance, regularly makes announcements together with slides encouraging the people to tune down the volume of their TV and hi-fi sets. The police also often acts against persons making excessive noise at unreasonable hours.

    Mr Speaker, Sir, there is no quick and easy answer to the problem of noise pollution in Singapore. Community noise is a particularly difficult problem due to our high population density and the cultural background of our people. However, it can be minimised by inculcating in our citizens a greater sense of civic consciousness and consideration for others. At the present moment, there are no plans to introduce further legislation to provide for anti-noise measures.

PSA ENTRY PASSES FOR STEVEDORES
(Extension of age limit)


    2. Mr Tan Cheng San asked the Prime Minister whether he will consider extending the age limit of stevedores to 65 years for the issue of Port of Singapore Authority passes.

     Mr Lim Kim San (for the Prime Minister): Mr Speaker, Sir, all stevedores, employed either by the PSA or private firms, require a pass from the PSA to work at the wharves.

    The work of a stevedore requires heavy physical exertion and quick reflexes. Those who are over 60 years of age are generally slow in their physical responses. This could endanger their own safety and the safety of those who are working with them.

SAVE ELECTRICITY CAMPAIGN
(Response)


    8. Mr Eugene Yap Giau Cheng asked the Minister for the Environment (a) who are the largest single group of energy users in Singapore; (b) is this group responding well to the "Save Electricity Campaign" recently launched by the Public Utilities Board, and (c) are there any other deterrent measures that the Board intends to adopt to discourage this group from further wastage of electricity.

     Mr Lim Kim San: Mr Speaker, Sir, electricity consumers can be grouped under three headings. They are industrial, domestic, commercial and other users. The industrial users form the largest group. They use 50% of the electricity generated, followed by commercial and other users, 33%, and domestic users, 17%. time before we can compile the necessary statistics to make a comparative study.

Time, therefore, is required for such modifications, and savings in power can only be seen after the modifications have been effected.
