These companies are subject to the provisions of the Elected President in the use of their accumulated reserves. They are also required to submit yearly financial statements to the President.

SINGAPORE ONE

    Mr Lee Yock Suan:

    The Singapore ONE pilot was launched in June 1997 and is still in its pilot phase.

    Households can access Singapore ONE through two network operators: Singapore Telecom (SingTel) and Singapore Cable Vision (SCV). Island-wide service is not available at the moment, but both operators are upgrading their systems with a view towards island-wide service by end 1998. RAIN SHELTERS UNDER FLYOVERS FOR MOTORCYCLISTS


    18. Mr Chuang Shaw Peng asked the Minister for Communications whether his Ministry will consider rain shelters under flyovers to provide relief to motorcyclists during heavy downpour and to enhance road safety.

    Mr Mah Bow Tan:

    Motorcyclists usually congregate and wait under flyovers on the left lane during heavy downpour. Hence, LTA has constructed rain shelters under flyovers for motorcyclists to take cover during heavy downpour. These are concrete bays located away from the main traffic to ensure the safety of the motorcyclists and other motorists. LTA will extend such shelters to all flyovers where space permits.

    LTA will be improving the existing rain shelters, such as the one along East Coast Parkway at the Tanjong Katong Flyover, by expanding the bays to allow them to accommodate more motorcyclists. LTA will explore how to overcome these constraints without compromising the safety of the motorists.

    There are currently 15 such rain shelters. Two more will be added at the flyovers at the junction of the Seletar and Tampines Expressways.
