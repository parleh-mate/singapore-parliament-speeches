    The Minister for Education (Mr Yong Nyuk Lin): Mr Speaker, Sir, last Thursday, 14th December, 1961, a delegation of seven, purporting to represent an organisation styled the "All-Singapore Chinese Secondary Students/Parents Association" called on me. I met them for about two hours together with my Parliamentary Secretary and two senior officials of my Ministry. Although there was no mention of this meeting in the English Press, both the Sin Chew Jit Poh and the Nanyang Siang Pau issues of 15th December, 1961, carried a report of the Press conference which they gave after the interview, and there are indications that this organisation will persist in its agitation over the recent examination boycott issue. Also, since the new

    On Wednesday, 13th December, I received a registered letter in Chinese from a certain Tan Kong Eng stating that a delegation of seven representatives of the "All-Singapore Chinese Secondary Students/Parents Association" would call on me on 14th of December at 10 a.m. to discuss - this, Sir, is the English equivalent: I quote - "matters concerning the change of system in the Chinese secondary schools with a view to arriving at an amicable settlement". The letter said that as the matter concerned the development of Chinese education, the Minister should spare some time for discussion though he might be very busy. After the interview, it was agreed that they would go back to re-discuss what had transpired at this interview and that they could come back again for another discussion, if necessary. I advised them that, in the meantime, it would be desirable to avoid issuing Press statements until the matter was resolved to mutual satisfaction.

    But, Sir, I see in the 15th December, 1961, issue of both the Sin Chew Jit Poh and the Nanyang Siang Pau that a Press conference was held immediately after the interview, it was reported inter alia - here again, Sir, I quote the English equivalent:

    'We wish to reiterate the nature of our representation. We would seriously point out that this delegation was created by the "All Singapore Chinese Secondary Students/Parents Association" to maintain and protect Chinese education, to look after the personal welfare of our children, to find a reasonable solution to the problem of changing the Chinese secondary school system. These are the responsibilities entrusted to us by over 2,000 parents.'
