    The Minister for Finance (Dr Goh Keng Swee): Mr Deputy Speaker, Sir, the Member has asked for information as to why it is necessary to transfer this large sum of money from the Consolidated Fund to the Development Fund. This is a fairly reasonable request for information, Mr Deputy Speaker, Sir. So I shall try to enlighten him. There are two main funds operated by the Government. The first is the Consolidated Fund. This is the Fund in which all revenues of the State are kept. From this Consolidated Fund all payments of Government Departments are paid out: running of Ministries, for staff, public services, and all the other items of expenditure in the Main Estimates which we passed the day before yesterday. That is the Consolidated Fund. In other words, it is a fund which deals with the operating expenses of the Government and keeps the current revenues of the Government. In other words, it is our current account.

    The other fund is the Development Fund. All the projects for capital expenditure, such as the Development Estimates which this Assembly passed yesterday, are paid out of monies of the Development Fund. The Development Fund gets its income from three principal sources. The first is loans which we raise each year in Singapore. The second is the interest on the earnings of the Development Fund. The third source comes from allocations from the Consolidated Fund to the Development

Column: 729

Fund. As Members will know, each year sums of money are transferred from the Consolidated Fund to the Development Fund. The purpose of this item in the Supplementary Estimates is to transfer this amount of money, namely, $200 million, from the Consolidated Fund to the Development Fund to meet expenditures in the State's development plan. So the short answer as to what the money is going to be used for is that it will be used to pay for expenditure on the projects which this Assembly approved yesterday.

    Now, why is it $200 million? If the Member will read the notes attached to this subhead, he will find this in the last sentence: "The Consolidated Fund now stands at $288 million which is much in excess of normal requirements...." Normally we want something like $60 million in the Consolidated Fund. That is quite ample to meet Government's expenditure in its day-to-day operations. But the Consolidated Fund has stood at this very large figure as a result of surpluses accumulated in previous years. It is necessary to transfer these surpluses in a lump sum now. Members will recollect that in 1961, we made a transfer of $80 million. Today, with a transfer of $200 million, we will have transferred $280 million of previous years' surpluses into the Development Fund. In 1959 we transferred $20 million from the Consolidated Fund to the Development Fund, and from 1960 to 1963 in each year we transferred $30 million. So the total of all these monies transferred from the Consolidated Fund to the Development Fund stands at something like $420 million. This is the surplus which we have referred to every now and then. Obviously there is no point in letting the money remain in the Consolidated Fund.
