Besides direct taxes on income and revenue, there are many areas of indirect taxes in Singapore, including various transfer payments to the Government in the form of levies and import duties. We have GST, COEs, import duties, ARF, maid levy, foreign workers levy, property tax, water conservation tax, cess tax, etc. Even the method of computing corporate tax ensures that companies usually pay higher than 27% because companies normally have other companies under them as subsidiaries and when they lose money they are not allowed to offset against the subsidiaries which make money.     If the Government needs the money, I do not think we have any quarrel with it in getting so much money into their coffers. But the question is: does it need the money? The huge surpluses of last year's plus the previous years' tell us a definite no.
