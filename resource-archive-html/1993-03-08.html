On tax reduction and rebates, I feel that we should look at it from a macro point of view so that we will realise that tax reform is part of our entire economic strategy and the main purpose is to assist Singapore in maintaining its competitiveness in the world market.

    First, on the benefits for the lower-income families. I feel that these rebates are necessary. Of course, I realise the benefits of shifting from direct taxes to indirect taxes. However, without all these measures of tax reduction and rebates, I do not think I would support the implementation of GST. Now I believe when the Government offers all these rebates and compensation, it would help the lower-income families to cushion the effect they will have to face with the introduction of GST. In Appendices E and F of the Finance Minister's Budget Speech, he had given a few concrete examples to illustrate how the lower-income families will benefit from all these rebates.

    Example 1. For households living in 1-room rented flats, with a family income of $500 per month, the average GST
