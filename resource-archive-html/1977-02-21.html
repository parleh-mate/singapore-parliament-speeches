ORAL ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS

ILLEGAL TRAWLING IN SERANGOON RIVER
AND THE JOHORE STRAITS


    1. Mr Ng Kah Ting asked the Minister for National Development and Communications whether he is aware of illegal trawling with boats powered by outboard motors in the Serangoon River and the Johore Straits which is seriously affecting the livelihood of fishermen in the area, and what steps he proposes to take to safeguard the livelihood of those fishermen.

    The Senior Minister of State for National Development (Dr Tan Eng Liang) (for the Minister for National Development and Communications): Mr Speaker, Sir, the Primary Production Department is aware of the illegal trawling activities in the Serangoon River and Johore Straits. The Primary Production Department with the assistance of the Marine Police, have been conducting enforcement action in this area. In 1976, the Department conducted a total of 55 operations. Four persons were arrested for illegal trawling along the Straits of Johore over the last three months, and legal proceedings are being instituted against them.

    As a deterrent measure, the Fisheries Act was amended in 1973 to provide mandatory jail sentence for illegal trawlers.

    The Department will continue to operate enforcement patrols against the illegal trawlers.

(Expansion of)


    2. Mr Ng Kah Ting asked the Minister for National Development and Communications what measures he proposes to take to encourage the expansion of otter trawling and if his Ministry will consult with the Ministry of Defence with a view to stepping up patrols by the Republic of Singapore Navy to protect our otter

trawling fleet from harassment by pirates on the high seas in the vicinity of Horsburgh Lighthouse.

    Dr Tan Eng Liang (for the Minister for National Development and Communications): Mr Speaker, Sir, otter trawling is one of the methods of fishing used by our fishermen. The Primary Production Department is also encouraging the fishermen to use other methods of fishing. However, the expansion of otter trawling is limited due to the lack of fishing ground and the lack of interest among our youths to take up fishing as they do not like the difficult life at sea.

    The Primary Production Department used to run a Fisheries Training School with UNDP assistance. From a survey conducted on more than 100 trainees who have completed a one-year training course, only 8% were found to be employed on board fishing vessels. The Training School has been discontinued since June 1976.

    The Primary Production Department maintains close liaison with the Republic of Singapore Navy and the Marine Police on the protection of our fishing vessels against piracy. Naval vessels have been deployed to conduct anti-piracy patrols in the vicinity of Horsburgh Lighthouse. This has resulted in a reduction in the number of piracy incidents in the area. In 1975, there were 25 reported harassment cases. In 1976, only seven cases were reported.
