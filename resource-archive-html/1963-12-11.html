COMMISSION OF INQUIRY TO INQUIRE INTO MISMANAGEMENT
OF UNION FUNDS BY PRO-COMMUNIST LEADERS OF THREE
UNIONS
(Statement by the Minister for Labour)


2.32 p.m.

    Mr Jek Yeun Thong: Mr Speaker, Sir, honourable Members will recollect that seven trade unions had their registration cancelled by the Registrar of Trade Unions on 30th October, 1963. The ground for cancellation was that these unions were being used for purposes inconsistent with their objects and rules and had taken part in Communist united front activity prejudicial to the good order and security of the

Column: 306

State. Before cancellation, these seven unions were given two months' notice on 28th August, 1963, to show cause why their registration should not be cancelled. Shortly after serving notice, official information was received by the Registrar of Trade Unions that leaders of three of the seven unions, namely, the Singapore General Employees' Union, the Singapore Business Houses Employees' Union and the Singapore Bus Workers' Union, in furtherance of their plan to stir up political unrest and to further the Communist united front campaign against merger and Malaysia, had met on the 1st and 3rd of September and decided to withdraw large sums of money from the bank accounts of the unions they controlled to implement their illegal plan.

    Honourable Members will recall that on the 3rd of September, His Excellency the Yang di-Pertuan Negara proclaimed the dissolution of the previous Assembly and everybody was aware that general elections would take place soon. In order to protect the funds of the unions, which rightly belong to the workers, from being misused, the Registrar immediately advised the banks to withhold withdrawal of any funds from the accounts of these unions. Despite this action by the Registrar, two unions, namely, the Singapore General Employees' Union and the Singapore Business Houses Employees' Union, however, managed to withdraw from the Bank of China $30,000 from their accounts between the period of 5th and 12th of October, 1963. In one instance, the Singapore General Employees' Union withdrew a sum of $13,500 on 12th October, 1963. When this information came to the knowledge of the Registrar, he sent an urgent memorandum requesting the acting President, the Secretary-General and the Treasurer of the union to see him on 14th October to explain why the withdrawals were made.

    On 14th of October, the three union officials accompanied by their legal adviser, Mr T. T. Rajah, called at the Registrar's Office. They admitted they had withdrawn three sums of $4,500

Column: 307

from the union's bank account with the Bank of China on 12th October, 1963. They explained that the money was handed over to Mr T. T. Rajah to pay Messrs. S. T. Bani, Fong Swee Suan and Sandra Woodhull, the so-called champions of the workers, as a form of ex-gratia reward for services rendered to the unions in the past few years. According to them, the payments were authorised by the union's Executive Committee at a meeting held on 10th October. An official note was then sent on 16th October by the Registrar to the three union officials and to Mr T. T. Rajah warning them not to make the payments as they would be acting contrary to the objects and rules of the union and that such payments are illegal under the law. In his reply to the Registrar, on 17th October, Mr T. T. Rajah stated that as Messrs. Fong Swee Suan, S. T. Bani and S. Woodhull had never asked for a cent from the union and as they were not consulted on the payment of an ex-gratia allowance, he had advised the union to set aside the sum held by him for disbursement for other purposes. In the same reply, however, Mr T. T. Rajah did mention that Messrs. Fong Swee Suan, S. T. Bani and S. Woodhull were given an allowance of $200 per month for pocket expenses. Registrar on the 13th of November, through Mr T. T. Rajah, that they had acted on his advice and had distributed the $13,500 to those members of the union who were unemployed.     Honourable Members will see from what I have recounted that here is a clear example of how unions under the control of a group of unscrupulous, anti-national, pro-Communist elements can sabotage genuine attempts by the Government to protect trade union funds in the interests of the workers. From the various complaints received from members of this union, Government has ample evidence which indicates that unions controlled by pro-Communist elements had utilised their funds to support the Communist united front campaign against merger and Malaysia.

    In view of the concern over the misuse of union funds and the disappearance of the union official connected therewith, the workers and the public in general would naturally like to know the actual state of the financial affairs in these unions. The Government therefore consider it essential to set up a Commission of Inquiry to inquire into the mismanagement of union funds by the pro-Communist leaders of these three unions. It is proposed to appoint Mr Tann Wee Kian of the Legal Service to be the Commissioner with the following terms of reference:
