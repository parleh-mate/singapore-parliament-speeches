LEAKAGES OF EXAMINATION PAPERS
(Statement by the Minister for Education)


10.10 a.m.

    Mr Chew Swee Kee: Mr Speaker, Sir, at the December sitting of this Assembly I made a statement on the English Secondary Schools Entrance Examination leakage and the Government Chinese Senior Middle III Examination leakage and promised to make a fuller statement on the Chinese Examination leakage, the investigation of which was not completed at that time. A thorough investigation of the administrative arrangements for the setting of papers and the printing has revealed the following facts:-

    (1) The seven question papers were set by seven different persons, five of whom were in the Ministry, one in an English secondary school and one in the Teachers' Training College.

    (2) The seven question papers were given to a professor from Nanyang University for the purpose of moderation by certain professors in the University.

    (3) The seven question papers were moderated by six professors of Nanyang University, each of them dealing with only one paper with the exception of one who dealt with two papers.

    (4) On the return of the question papers from Nanyang University, the persons who set the questions made certain amendments to the questions and in one paper which went up to Nanyang University in English the questions were

Column: 1994

translated into Chinese in the Ministry. The final form of the questions was determined in my Ministry only after the questions had returned from Nanyang University, and thus the professors up at the University did not know the final form of the questions. The questions that leaked out were in their final form.

    (5) All these question papers were sent to the Government Printing Office for printing. The proofs were checked by the setters themselves and returned to the Printing Office for final printing.

    (6) The packing of the printed papers was supervised by three officials of the Ministry at the Printing Office and brought back personally by them to the Ministry.

    (7) During this period from the setting of the first questions until the delivery of the printed papers, they were kept in the custody of the Assistant Examinations Officer.

    (8) The Government Printer has accounted for all the papers printed by him either in proof or in final form.

    The revelation of the above facts should have brought about the exposure of the culprit or culprits, but unfortunately this has been made impossible by the attitude of the witnesses.

    The appeal to students and others to come forward to give information with regard to the leakage has been disappointing. Although many pupils who had been in possession of the questions had been interrogated, none of them was prepared to speak the whole truth. Invariably the final answers were either that they obtained the questions anonymously through the telephone, through the post or from collective copying in a coffee shop or at some other place. Even those who had come forward to surrender some of the questions before the examination were not prepared to give further information. They said that their object was to prove to the Government that there had been a leakage of questions so that the dishonest students would not get their qualifications through foul means. KAMPONG TIONG BAHRU FIRE
(Statement by the Minister for Labour and Welfare)


10.15 a.m.

    Tun Lim Yew Hock: Mr Speaker, Sir, as stated in my reply to the Member for Tiong Bahru, I wish to make a statement now on the disastrous fire which occurred on Friday, 13th February, and devastated a large section of Kampong Tiong Bahru, rendering some 5,000 persons homeless and causing considerable loss and damage to property.

    Happily only one person died although there were 58 casualties, nine of whom were admitted to hospital. Hon. Members will have this tragic event still fresh in their minds and I need not go over the harrowing details of this tragedy which has called forth the sympathy, not only of the whole of Singapore, but also from our brothers in the Federation and Brunei and sympathisers from the United Kingdom. In this account, I intend to confine myself purely to the sober statistical details of this fire, the largest Singapore has experienced, and to an account of the measures which Government has taken or intends to provide.

    In accordance with the scheme for undertaking emergency measures whenever a fire or other calamity occurs, apart from measures taken to fight the fire and provide immediate medical care and attention to the victims, the Government set up a relief centre under the control of the Social Welfare Department at the Kim Seng Road School. The Social Welfare Department staff and voluntary workers manned the relief

Column: 1997

centre day and night. Donations of food and clothing and goods immediately poured in from the ever generous public and from voluntary associations in Singapore.

    From these donations the Singapore Council of Social Services have to date distributed $154,140 in cash to the fire victims through the Social Welfare Department while that Department itself has paid out $26,060. The Singapore Council of Social Services has spent a further $10,000 on feeding, the Social Welfare Department another $2,000 on equipment, the Public Works Department have spent $12,500 on works carried out, while health services and milk were provided by the Ministry of Health.

    While these measures provided immediate relief to the victims, it was necessary to consider the no less urgent problem of their future accommodation.

    On the morning following the fire, a team from the Estates Department of the Singapore Improvement Trust, by liaison with the Social Welfare Department, obtained basic information in connection with rehousing requirements. Including late registrations and additions made subsequently, it was necessary to consider rehousing 115 single persons and 920 families amounting to approximately 4,500 persons in all. On Monday, 16th February, Government directed the Singapore Improvement Trust to provide temporary accommodation for the fire victim families at Kallang Airport Estate and to prepare schemes for the immediate redevelopment of the area devastated by the fire. On the following day, Sir, I announced the Government's decision that the victims of the Kampong Tiong Bahru fire would be accommodated free of charge for three months and that a large number of low-cost flats would be built to rehouse all the victims.

    The move of all persons living within the school premises and the majority of those who have found accommodation

Column: 1998

outside the school was completed on Thursday, 26th February. During the 13 days, from the time the Trust was first directed to take part in the operation, 779 units of accommodation were allocated and occupied by approximately 900 families and single person groups. The total move involved more than 4,000 persons. Lettings of 779 units in normal times would take some two months. The organization and effort contributed by the staff who condensed the volume of work into a few days has been possible only because of continued working by the staff on the site until late hours each day.

    Now I must come to the Government's intentions in regard to the redevelopment of the fire site itself. It is considered that only a planned redevelopment of the site with permanent housing should be carried out and that haphazard rebuilding with attap or timber barrack buildings should be strongly resisted and prevented. Government has now accordingly gazetted for acquisition the full area of the fire site itself together with additional lands around it for satisfactory redevelopment. The proposals are that the fire site should be developed by the erection of 240 two-room single-storey units and 500 two-room units in multi-storey blocks. The single-storey units will be constructed to a simple but durable structure by the use of wood wool slabs and concrete hollow blocks. Individual water supply and electricity would be laid on to each unit. The overall cost of these two-room units, including a proportion of site work, would be $2,500. Such two-room units could be let complete to larger families or be split to individual rooms for allocation to small families. The redevelopment of the higher portions of the site, having good load bearing capacity would be with seven-storey buildings containing units of almost identical plan to the single storey structures but having balcony access. Such units could again be let out complete or as two individual rooms.

    It is estimated that the cost of 500 units of the two-room units in the multi-storey blocks will be approximately $5,000 each or $2,500 per single room.

    Sanction will be sought at the next sitting of this Assembly for the provision of additional monies amounting to approximately $3.3 million for this development. In the meantime, the Singapore Improvement Trust is being authorised to proceed immediately with site works in anticipation of Legislative Assembly approval.

    I should like to end this statement by voicing the thanks of this Government and, I am sure, the victims of this fire will wish me to do so on their behalf, to all persons who worked long and devoted hours to alleviate the plight of the afflicted victims, and to the very willing co-operation and assistance rendered by all bodies and organizations, both public and private, from the City Council and the Singapore Improvement Trust to the Council of Social Services and other relief organizations. Last but not least, I must not forget the very generous public of Singapore and our friends in the Federation, Brunei and the United Kingdom whose donations and contributions and more than this, the thought behind these donations and contributions, have warmed our hearts and brought solace to the unfortunate victims.

There will be no change in the freedom of movement between the two territories along the Causeway other than in the case of those citizens who are specifically prohibited by order of either Government.
