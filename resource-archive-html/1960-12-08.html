    The long Budget Statement delivered by the Minister for Finance in this House on 29th November, 1960, gave a penetrating and objective analysis of the financial and economic situation confronting the self-governing State at present and in the years ahead. In the colonial era and the era of the Lim Yew Hock Government, there has never been such a Minister for Finance who could have given such a comprehensive and substantial analysis of the financial and economic situation confronting the people of Singapore. From the economic point of view, had the Minister for Finance given us in his Statement approximate figures of the profits derived locally by foreign enterprises in the past one year, and had he told us what percentage of their profits they were prepared to re-invest locally, then his speech would have been more complete, though perhaps we have no such need. But anyway, we must thank the Minister for Finance for his great effort in handling the finance and economy of Singapore in the past one year. He has pointed out to the people of Singapore the right way of overcoming the financial and economic difficulties through their own effort. The people can see, from his review of the conditions of economy and trade in our State this year, that in the 1½ years of office, the P.A.P. has not only brought about an unprecedented stable political situation but also a new situation of increased trade and expansion in production after a short period of economic standstill. Our Friends on the Opposition strongly put forth their propaganda that when the P.A.P. gained power, factories would be closed and trade would come to a standstill. This phenomenon has not come about at all. Of course, we have no reason at all to be too satisfied, because the increase in trade and the expansion in export still cannot raise the living standard of the workers and labourers. Our rapidly increasing population gives us many problems which are difficult to settle.

    However, I think it is necessary to emphasise the realistic policy adopted by the P.A.P. Government in dealing with

the economic problem. The P.A.P. believe in a society where there is no exploitation. According to the Budget Statement of the Minister for Finance, our industrialisation programme will be carried mainly through the system of private and free enterprise. Thus honest capitalists need not worry about their capital investments not being protected by political measures.

    The working classes of Singapore, as a whole, have fully understood the situation in Singapore and have expressed their tolerance and wisdom in their struggle for better living conditions. During the past year, without their co-operation, the manufacturing industries and trade could not have expanded. Generally speaking, the working classes are not to be blamed for industrial unrest under the free enterprise system, except a few of them.
