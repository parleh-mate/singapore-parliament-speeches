ELECTION OF PRESIDENT OF THE REPUBLIC OF SINGAPORE


2.30 p.m.

     The Prime Minister: Mr Speaker, Sir, I beg to move the Motion standing in my name,

    That this Parliament, in accordance with Article 1 of the Constitution, elects Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore for a further term of four years to commence immediately after the expiry of his present term of office.

    Sir, it was eight years ago, in December 1970, that I first moved his election as President. He was then 63. He had a distinguished career as a professor and practitioner of Obstetrics & Gynaecology. He

Column: 56

brought to his office a fine intellect and a quiet dignity.

    He keeps himself abreast with all important matters of state. All Cabinet papers and other important subjects go up to him so that he is aware of the background to the decisions he is required by the Constitution to endorse. He also undertakes his social and protocol functions with conscientious interest, never treating them as perfunctory chores. He has done us proud and has been an asset to Singapore these past eight years. No distinguished visitor who has called on him has failed to notice the compliment he has paid him by really knowing about him, his country, and his major interests.

    When I asked him in April this year if he would serve as President for a third term, he

Column: 57

asked for time to consider it. Characteristically, he wanted to satisfy himself that he could and would discharge his duties to the high standards he sets himself. It was after careful deliberation that he told me in October this year that he was prepared to undertake another term. He said he would do his best. I have no doubts that he will.

    I have less hesitation in commending his re-election to the office, than he had in accepting the nomination. For me, the question was simple. Is there a better man for the job? There is none. He is the best. However, the question he put to himself was different. Could he discharge his duties in the next four years as well as he had in the last eight?

    It took time to persuade and convince him that whilst his energy and vigour at 71 may not be what it was at 63 when he first assumed office, nevertheless, it was more than adequate, given the experience he has acquired, and the routine he has worked out for himself. I am happy that I was able to persuade him to continue.

    I have no doubts that the habits of a life-time will not change. It is part of his nature to apply himself thoroughly to his work.

    Mr Speaker, Sir, I believe some thoughtfulness on the part of the Government can lighten the physical demands of the office. These are the adjustments each of us has to make as the years go by. It is called growing old gracefully, a phrase forged by poetic license. If we are fortunate to have the right mental approach, we learn to accept these changes without resentment. If we are more fortunate, like the President is, we shall have members of the family and friends who help and encourage us to make these changes as we soldier on.

    Sir, I beg to move.

    Question proposed.
2.35 p.m.

     Mr Yeo Choo Kok (Delta)( In Mandarin): Sir, I rise to support the motion moved by the Prime Minister.

Column: 58



    We all know that in order to achieve a good record in any matter one is dealing with, one has to show excellent performance. Today, Parliament is creating a new record by electing Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore for a third term. This demonstrates clearly and speaks well for the wisdom and ability shown by President Sheares ever since his installation in the presidency, and these qualities have received unanimous acclamation and admiration by the whole nation.

    Our Government is well known for its fairplay, integrity, acumen and ability to govern. It is indeed not an easy matter for one to be elected as President for a third time by such a Government.

    Disregarding his personal health and undaunted by the laborious tasks of a President, the resumption of office with its onerous duties once more by President Sheares manifests a selfless and resolute spirit which is exactly the kind of responsive spirit we need to meet every kaleidoscopic change of this ever-changing world of ours. With such a superior spirit, topped with extensive learning and rich experiences, President Sheares will no doubt continue to project a good image and be an example for our nationals. His continuance in the President's office for a third term has been what all have been looking forward to.

    Once more, I would like to express my support for the motion moved by the Prime Minister.

     Encik Sidek bin Saniff (Kolam Ayer)( In Malay): Mr Speaker, Sir, when the Honourable Prime Minister moved a motion on 30th December, 1970, to elect the illustrious Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore, upon the return to the mercy of God of the First President, the late Tun Yusof Ishak, he had informed Parliament and the nation concerning the character and personality of the President-designate.

    Among his other attributes, he is a doctor eminent in his own specialty. He is kind and endowed with a courteous disposition. He is

Column: 59

modest and friendly by nature and habit, and has given generously of his time to public duties.

    The truth of the biographical notes has been proven when I observed his demeanour at ceremonies that he graced.

    Further, during my two years' stint as a Member of Parliament, I have been presented with many more opportunities to know him. I am now able to observe him at close quarters. His unobtrusiveness and integrity have become even more conspicuous. Indeed, his sincerity is not only visible but can be felt and experienced when we shake hands with him. This is even more so in his utterances.

    I think it is this sincerity which is ingrained in him that has become a dominant source of strength which has gradually brought the State to progress and national unity.

    This is analogous to the reply given by the Prophet Muhammad (Peace be on him) to an Arab Bedouin, who asked, "What is the strongest substance in the world?" to which the Prophet replied, "The mountains". "Anything stronger than the mountains?". "Steel" (which can lay waste the earth and rock) replied the Prophet. "Stronger than steel?" "Water" (which can crush steel). "Stronger than water?" "Air" (which can hurl water in every direction). "Stronger than air?" "Sincerity", replied the Prophet. Sincerity can overcome, fracture and stabilise all forms of superficial strengths.

    I am confident that all of us in this House as well as the nation as a whole are aware that history has recorded that during his tenure of office as President, he has invested the office of Head of State with an air of dignity and integrity. This is vital. In doing so, he has also enhanced the popular image of the nation and people as a whole.

    On this note, I support with whole-hearted sincerity the motion moved by the Prime Minister to re-elect Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of our beloved Republic for a third term.

     Dr Ang Kok Peng (Buona Vista): Mr Speaker, Sir, allow me the privilege and pleasure to support the motion moved by the Prime Minister to re-elect Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore.

    Dr Sheares has discharged the duties as President with distinction; he has brought to this eminent office dignity, decorum, sincerity and keen interest.

    In spite of the heavy demands made on him by the affairs of the State, he has also served with diligence as the Chancellor of the University of Singapore since June 1971. The University has benefited much from his rich experience and wide knowledge and the staff and students have great admiration for his guidance and warm regard for his gentleness.

    No doubt the same intellectual excellence that had earlier distinguished him as an outstanding professor of obstetrics and gynaecology coupled with his deep and profound understanding of human nature has contributed much to his able leadership as Head of State.

    His devotion and service to the Nation over the years have inspired in us a complete confidence in his ability to continue discharging the duties of President in a manner befitting the highest office in our land.

    Mr Speaker, Sir, I therefore whole-heartedly second the motion re-electing Dr Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore for a further term. In doing so, I am certain that all hon. Members will join me in assuring him of our continued support and close cooperation.

    Question put, and agreed to.

    Resolved,

    That this Parliament, in accordance with Article 1 of the Constitution, elects Dr Benjamin Henry Sheares as President of the Republic of Singapore for a further term of four years to commence immediately after the expiry of his present term of office.
