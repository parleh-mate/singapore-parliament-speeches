     The Minister of State for Labour (Mr Sia Kah Hui) (for the Minister for Communications and Minister for Labour): Mr Speaker, Sir, last year a total of 484 new unionized cases were referred to the Labour Relations Division for conciliation. Added to the 142 cases that have been brought forward from 1979, there was a total of 626 cases.     The time taken to conciliate disputes vary. It depends on the nature of the dispute. The average duration for the resolution of a dispute on proposals for a collective agreement is about three months, while a grievance dispute takes about a week. However, in terms of actual time spent, each case takes less than one man-day on the average.

     Mr Liew Kok Pun (Boon Teck): Sir, may I ask the Minister of State what steps is his Ministry taking to speed up the conciliation process?
