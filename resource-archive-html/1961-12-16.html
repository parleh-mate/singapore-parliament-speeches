    The Minister for Education (Mr Yong Nyuk Lin): Mr Speaker, Sir, I beg to move, "That the Bill be now read a Second time."

    The University of Malaya was established on 8th October, 1949, as a national institution to serve the higher educational needs of Singapore and the Federation of Malaya. The origins of the University could be traced back to the founding in Singapore of the King Edward VII College of Medicine in 1905 and Raffles College in 1929. Both these Colleges gradually expanded to serve the educational needs of the increasing population of Malaya until World War II intervened in 1942.

    In 1947 a Commission was appointed under the chairmanship of Sir Alexander Carr-Saunders to inquire into and make recommendations concerning university education in Malaya. As a result of the

Column: 2340

recommendations of this Commission, the University of Malaya was founded in 1949. Although the Commission recommended that the two Colleges should form the nucleus of the new University, it also anticipated the rapid demand for higher education in a swiftly expanding population of the two territories, and so it recommended that the new University should consider moving into South Johore in order that future expansion would not be hindered for lack of space. However, this did not materialise because of the enormous cost involved, and the scheme had to be abandoned in 1954.

    In 1957 a Commission of Inquiry headed by Dr R. S. Aitken was set up by the Governments of Singapore and the Federation, this time at the request of the University, in order to review the constitution, working, and finances of the University in the light of experience and rapid expansion of the last seven years, and of the prospective expansion in the near future, including the plan for developing the University in Kuala Lumpur, and to make recommendations. Arising from the Commission's Report and resulting from the recommendations of the two joint Committees on Constitution and Finance appointed by the two Governments, legislation was passed in November 1958 providing for the continuance of the University of Malaya as a single university but with the establishment of two largely autonomous Divisions of equal status, one in Singapore and the other at Kuala Lumpur. This legislation came into effect on 15th January, 1959.

    The University's running expenses continued to be met from grants from the Governments of Singapore and the Federation of Malaya, hitherto made on a quinquennial basis in the ratio 40 : 60, on the basis of student numbers from the two territories. But in 1959 they were amended to a triennial basis for the period 1959-61 and the ratio changed to a basis of 1: 2. It was also expected that by 1962 each Government would be responsible for looking after its own Division and at the same time contributing towards the annual recurrent cost of

the other Division in respect of its students attending courses not available in its own Division. Capital grants, however, would continue to be made on an equal basis during the triennium.

    In May 1960 the Governments of Singapore and the Federation of Malaya indicated their intention to establish autonomous separate national universities in Singapore and the Federation by 1962. Steps were accordingly taken in both territories to redraft new constitutions for each Division to found two separate national universities. At the request of the Divisional Council of the University of Malaya (Singapore Division), a joint Government/University committee of seven were appointed in June this year for this purpose. And the present draft Bill was submitted to both the Divisional Senate and Divisional Council for their views. I might add that the Singapore Joint Government/University Committee were in the happy position of being able to use the draft Bill of the Federation's Joint Government/University Committee in its deliberations. The present Bill has also incorporated all material points suggested by both the Divisional Senates and Council of the University of Malaya (Singapore Division). Our Bill is also closely in line with the main provision of the University of Malaya Act which was passed in the Federation Parliament this year.
