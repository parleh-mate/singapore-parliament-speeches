ORAL ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS

HDB VOID DECKS
(Toilet facilities for senior citizens)


    1. Mr Ng Kah Ting asked the Minister for National Development whether the Housing and Development Board will consider providing toilet facilities and more tables and chairs in the void decks of HDB estates for the use of senior citizens.

     The Minister of State for National Development (Dr Lee Boon Yang) (for the Minister for National Development): Mr Speaker, Sir, I am answering on behalf of my Minister.

    In new HDB estates, a kiosk with an attached toilet is provided to serve each precinct consisting of four to six blocks of flats. The toilet is manned and maintained by the kiosk operator who sells sundry items. It is a condition of the tenancy of such kiosks for the kiosk operator to open the toilet to the public for a fee. Residents can use such toilets. The HDB will not provide any other public toilets on the void deck because of maintenance problems.

    The HDB provides a Residents' Corner on the void deck of new apartment blocks. In each Residents' Corner, there is a partially enclosed space with a set of table and stools. Additional tables and stools can be provided if needed.

    In existing estates where there are no kiosks or Residents' Corners, the HDB can provide these facilities under the Repairs and Redecoration Programme.

     Mr Ng Kah Ting (Punggol): Sir, will the Minister of State consider asking the HDB to build kiosks in the existing estates so that such facilities can be provided?

     Dr Lee Boon Yang: Mr Speaker, Sir, as I have said in my main answer, HDB will consider such proposals.

PEDESTRIAN OVERHEAD BRIDGE AT ANG MO KIO AVENUE 6
(Provision of shelter)


    5. Mr Lau Ping Sum asked the Minister for National Development whether the pedestrian overhead bridge under construction at Ang Mo Kio Avenue 6 near the Yio Chu Kang Mass Rapid Transit station will be provided with shelter; and what will be the cost of providing such a shelter.

     Dr Lee Boon Yang (for the Minister for National Development): Mr Speaker, Sir, it is the Public Works Department's practice not to provide a covered overhead pedestrian bridge if it is not connected directly to buildings at both ends. The overhead pedestrian bridge under construction at Ang Mo Kio Avenue 6 near the Yio Chu Kang Mass Rapid Transit station is not connected to any building and will not therefore be provided cover.

    It would only make sense to provide a sheltered overhead pedestrian bridge if the bridge under construction is extended 70 metres to the Mass Rapid Transit station at one end and 20 metres to the nearest HDB flat (Block 636) at the other. Such a covered overhead pedestrian bridge will cost $422,000 as against the bridge under construction which will cost $238,000.

     Mr Lau Ping Sum (Yio Chu Kang): Sir, would the Minister of State consider in the longer-term, in order to upgrade the quality of life and to provide greater convenience to residents, to review this policy of providing shelter to all overhead pedestrian bridges?

     Dr Lee Boon Yang: Mr Speaker, Sir, PWD constantly re-assesses its designs for overhead pedestrian bridges. If there is a need and if it is cost-beneficial to improve the design and provide cover, then it would be considered.
