DISPOSAL OF BULKY REFUSE FROM HDB FLATS


    1. Mr Eric Cheong Yuen Chee asked the Minister for National Development what steps the Housing and Development Board is taking to expedite the removal of bulky refuse, e.g. disused cupboards, old mattresses and other household items, discarded at staircase landings and lift entrances which, besides posing a fire hazard, may be used as killer litter.

     The Minister for National Development (Mr Teh Cheang Wan): Mr Speaker, Sir, the HDB residents are responsible for bringing down their bulky household refuse such as furniture, refrigerators, washing machines, etc, from their flats to the nearest bin compound for final disposal at the dumping grounds. Instead, many residents are discarding their bulky refuse at lift entrances, staircase landings and other common areas within the blocks. The indiscriminate dumping not only causes inconvenience but also poses a danger to other residents. In order to prevent any mishap to residents, arrangement is being made by HDB to cart them away whenever they are found by HDB field staff in their routine rounds.

    To completely eradicate the problem of bulky refuse being discarded in the common areas, the HDB will need the full cooperation of residents. The Board has therefore organized "Junk Removal Days" jointly with the grassroots organizations to encourage residents to dispose of their bulky refuse in an orderly manner.

    Articles are also published in "Our Home" magazine and the mass media to raise residents' awareness of the dangers posed by the bulky refuse.

    Apart from these measures, the Board is also looking into the possibility of providing a service to the residents to remove their bulky refuse from homes at a charge.

HOUSING NEEDS OF THE AGED IN HDB ESTATES


    2. Mr Eric Cheong Yuen Chee asked the Minister for National Development, in view of the projected increase in the number of senior citizens residing in Housing and Development Board estates, what steps are being taken by the HDB to cater to the physical, social and recreational needs of these citizens.

     Mr Teh Cheang Wan: Mr Speaker, Sir, the Government has always been concerned about the housing needs of the aged. Various schemes have already been implemented by the HDB to provide accommodation for our senior citizens. They are:

    (a) Joint Ballotting Scheme. Implemented in 1976, this scheme allows married children and their parents to be allocated two or more units of adjoining flats. As at December 1985, 16,471 families have been allocated flats under this scheme.

    (b) Multi-tier Family Housing Scheme. This scheme encourages two or three generations of the same family to stay together in the same flat. Applicants under the scheme are granted three years' priority in allocation. Since its implementation in 1982, a total of 9,885 units have been allocated under the scheme. Another 1,874 applicants are on the waiting list, pending allocation as at December 1985.

    (c) Aged/Senior Citizen Scheme. Implemented in 1978, the objective of this scheme is to provide housing for the senior citizens who are single. Two or more single senior citizens can apply to purchase HDB flats jointly. As at December 1985, 405 units have been allocated under the scheme.

    The HDB will continue to monitor the demographic trends of Singapore. Adjustments will be made to existing schemes and new schemes will be introduced, if necessary, to respond to the changing needs of society.
