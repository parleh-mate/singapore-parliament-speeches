    3. Mr Lee Kuan Yew asked the Chief Minister whether he has received from London a draft of the Constitution of the new State of Singapore, and when other parties who participated in the London talks will receive copies of the draft for their approval.

    4. Mr Seah Peng Chuan asked the Chief Minister whether he will give this House an opportunity to debate the desirability of a proposed further all-Party Mission to London in connection with the proposed new Constitution for Singapore; and if he will give an estimate of the probable expense to be borne by this territory in connection with the proposed Mission.

    5. Mr Seah Peng Chuan asked the Chief Minister whether there has been correspondence between the Singapore Government and the Colonial Office in London arising out of the rejection by this Assembly last year of the anti-subversive clause in the Report of the Constitutional Conference held in London in March and April 1957 and whether he will present such correspondence to this House.

    The Chief Minister (Dr Lim Yew Hock): Mr Speaker, Sir, as there are a number of questions on today's Order Paper on the subject of the Constitutional Order in Council, with your permission I shall answer Nos. 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 at the same time.

    The constitutional instruments are still being drafted in the Colonial Office and I hope that the completed draft will be received here by the end of this month. I have myself recently discussed the drafting with Mr Melville of the Colonial Office during his visit to Singaport, and certain sections have been referred to our local experts for checking and revision. As soon as the completed draft is ready, I shall study it in conjunction with the members of the All-Party Delegation who were signatories to the conference report.

    The stand taken by the Assembly on the anti-subversive clause was reported to the Secretary of State. No correspondence between this Government and the Colonial Office was entered into on this matter, nor has this Government

Column: 18

entered into any correspondence with the Colonial Office on the drafting of the Constitution.

    The introduction of the new Constitution can be effected at any time this Assembly considers that the time has come to address the Governor to fix a date for the new Constitution in accordance with the agreement set out in paragraph 54 of the Report of the Singapore Constitutional Conference, 1957.

    I would invite the attention of hon. Members to that part of the Governor's speech delivered on 9th April which reads as follows:

    "The Government intends that the agreement with the United Kingdom Government on the form of the constitutional instruments should be made by the All-Party delegation which took part in the Constitutional discussion last year."
The constitutional instruments deal with the life and future of the new State of Singapore and cannot be framed to the satisfaction of local political Parties and the local Government on the one side, and the United Kingdom Government some 8,000 miles away, without delay and without the risk of losing that unity so desirable for such a vital business save by discussions by the parties concerned. I have therefore made arrangements with the Colonial Office for such discussions to take place in May, and I propose to invite the same members to London to finalise the constitutional instruments. The probable expense to Singapore is return air passages for six persons plus necessary incidentals.

    The desirability of visiting London for this purpose cannot be gainsaid and I do not consider it necessary that this Assembly should be given an opportunity to debate this matter.

FIRE AT KAMPONG KOO CHYE, GEYLANG: RELIEF AND RE-HOUSING OF VICTIMS


    1. Mr Lim Cher Kheng asked the Minister for Labour and Welfare whether he will make a statement on the recent fire at Kampong Koo Chye, Geylang, and what steps have been taken for the relief and re-housing of the victims of the fire.

    Dr Lim Yew Hock: The fire at Kampong Koo Chye (Lorong 3) started at about 4.30 p.m. on Saturday, 5th April, 1958 and was brought under control at about 7.30 p.m., by which time over 100 huts had been destroyed, rendering 379 families or 2,050 persons homeless. An emergency feeding scheme was immediately put into operation by the Department of Social Welfare at Sims Avenue Community Centre at 8 p.m. to feed those who were in need of food. Coffee with milk was also served. In the meantime, steps were taken to establish a Relief Centre at Geylang English School to house those who are without alternative accommodation. Blankets from the Civil Defence store were distributed to all the families in the Relief Centre. This Relief Centre is run by the Department of Social Welfare, whose officers are on duty day and night to attend to the needs of the fire victims, such as answering queries or calling for the ambulance in case of sickness or birth. On Sunday, 6th April, 1958 an emergency payment of $5 a person was made by the Department of Social Welfare. A total sum of $10,075 was expended.

    The Blue Cross Charitable Institution which has always worked hand in hand with the Department of Social Welfare after a fire, swung into action the next day, that is, Sunday, 6th April, 1958 and assumed full responsibility for feeding the fire victims twice a day. It also supplied tea, coffee and cold drinks to the victims throughout the day and night. In addition, it supplied household necessities such as tooth brush, tooth paste, soap, towels and so on to the victims.

    In view of the number of children, nurses from the Health Department of the City Council are on day and night duty looking after the feeding and care of the children. The St. John's Ambulance Brigade (since withdrawn), and the British Red Cross also did day and night duty in the Relief Centre. The Civil Defence personnel helped and supplied the necessary transport during the first and second day. The number of voluntary welfare workers that helped were too numerous to be mentioned.

    The response from the public to the appeal for donations in cash and kind was overwhelming. Rice and fresh food continued to be donated to the Relief Centre in large quantities and the amount of clothing received was far more than what the fire victims required. With the $5 a head from the Social Welfare Department included each fire victim had now received a sum of $82. At present, 296 families consisting of about 1,050 fire victims are accommodated in the Relief Centre at the Geylang English School. Cinema and other variety shows are put on for their entertainment.
