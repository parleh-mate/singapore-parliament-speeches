    1. Dr Lily Neo asked the Minister for Health if he will review (i) the policy of restricting the number of overseas universities for approved medical studies; (ii) the criteria for the recognition of overseas medical degrees; and (iii) the list of universities with degrees approved for registration in Singapore.

    The Minister for Health (Mr Lim Hng Kiang): Mr Speaker, Sir, the intake into the NUS medical school is currently 200 a year. Each year, we expect about 80 overseas graduates to be registered for practice in Singapore, including both foreign doctors and overseas trained Singaporeans. The 20 universities whose medical degrees are registrable with the Singapore Medical Council are in various developed countries, such as the UK, USA and Australia, so we have sufficient choice of graduates. This number also excludes the 160-odd doctors who are under temporary registration in Singapore at any one time.

    The overseas medical schools are top ranking institutions in their respective countries and have been assessed to be of a standard comparable to the NUS medical school, or better. The list is reviewed from time to time and updated. The present list remains relevant and serves our needs.

    Dr Lily Neo (Kreta Ayer-Tanglin): May I ask the Minister if we are in fact short of doctors here again and, if it is so, why are we adhering with the policy of restricting the number of recognised universities for medical students?

    Mr Lim Hng Kiang: When we debated the health policy in the White Paper in 1993, we made it quite clear that we want to prevent over supply of doctors and specialists in Singapore. Because if there are too many doctors and specialists, the demand for medical services will be inflated and healthcare costs will go up. So that has been the fundamental pillar of our policy. In the White Paper, we would control the output from NUS at 150 per year and also from foreign trained doctors at 50 per year. Because of the change in population policy, we have increased the intake in NUS to 200 and also the intake from foreign trained doctors from 50 to 80. So we have made the necessary adjustments to meet the higher population that we have. The situation is quite comfortable today.
