    Demand for water in Singapore has risen at an increasing rate in the last three years. In 1982, it grew by 3.5%; in 1983 by 5.1%; and in 1984 by 7.2%.

    There are three main reasons why the demand for water has shot up so rapidly. First, there was a large increase in the number of new households as more public housing and private condominium units were completed. Second, there was also a substantial increase in the number of hotel rooms. Third, there was increased construction activity, where there is considerable wastage. If we continue to consume water at the current rate, in 15 years, we will be using more water than all our reservoirs in Singapore and sources in Johore can ever supply.
