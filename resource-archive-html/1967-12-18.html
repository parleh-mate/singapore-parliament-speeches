(Increase of rates and payment facilities)


    1. Mr Ang Nam Piau asked the Minister for Culture and Social Affairs if the Government will consider increasing the rates of Public Assistance Allowances and if he will set up payment centres in all the 51 constituencies for the convenience of the recipients and to save them expense on transport in having to go to the Social Welfare Department in Havelock Road.

    The Minister for Culture and Social Affairs (Inche Othman Bin Wok): Mr Speaker, Sir, since the P.A.P. Government assumed office in June 1959, the rates of public assistance allowance given to the head of household have been increased twice from - $15 to $16 in September 1959, and again from $16 to $21 in January 1962. The allowance for the wife was also increased from $8 to $10 per month as from September 1959.

    As the existing rates of public assistance are the highest in this part of the world, it is not considered prudent in the interests of our economy to make further increases which can only be done if we raise more taxes, in 1966 a sum of $8,262,815.95 cents was paid to 22,241 families on the public assistance scheme.

    At present there are 41 public assistance payment centres situated in all parts of the island, apart from the payment centre at the Social Welfare Department at Havelock Road, in addition, there are mobile payment teams that go to the neighbouring islands, hospitals, homes for the aged, and to the individual homes of those recipients who are unable to get to the payment centres to receive their public assistance allowance. As these payment centres have been so located for the convenience of the recipients with a view to lessening their expense on transport
