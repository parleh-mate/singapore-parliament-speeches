    1. Mr Tay Boon Too asked the Minister for Education whether he is aware that many teachers, who do extra-curricular duties in Government and Government-aided schools, are given less teaching periods and have more free periods than teachers who do only teaching, and if he will look into the matter.

Column: 511



    The Minister of State for Education (Inche A. Rahim Ishak) (for the Minister for Education): Mr Speaker, Sir, I am aware that teachers who perform extra-curricular duties are given more free periods than other teachers wherever possible, and this is as it should be since extra-curricular duties entail a great deal of time both during and after school hours and can be more onerous than teaching in a class.     Little or no attention was given to the effect of the devaluation as a whole, on the future earnings of the Singapore workers. Sir, some have expressed gleefully, "The British-made cars would be cheaper by 15 per cent." So may it be.
