A & E DEPARTMENTS
(Screening of emergency cases)


    7. Dr Koh Lam Son asked the Acting Minister for Health what are the reasons for employing a nurse rather than a medical officer to screen emergency from non-emergency cases at the Accident & Emergency Departments.

     The Acting Minister for Health (Mr Yeo Cheow Tong): Mr Speaker, Sir, nurses are employed to screen patients at the Accident & Emergency Department as the screening process is a relatively routine function which nurses are competent to undertake. This relieves such duties from the doctors who can then devote their time attending to the true emergency cases.

    Screening is done by asking the patient for details of his or her symptoms and by visual assessment of the general condition of the patient. Where necessary, the nurses will measure the patient's temperature, pulse and blood pressure.

    The nurses employed for screening are experienced nurses who have been trained specifically and provided with guidelines to distinguish emergency from non-emergency cases.

    There is also close supervision of the nurses' work by the doctors. Should they encounter any doubt or difficulties in screening, they would immediately consult the doctors or refer the patients to them.
