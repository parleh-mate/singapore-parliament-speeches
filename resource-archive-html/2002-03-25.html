    The Leader of the House (Mr Wong Kan Seng): Mr Speaker, Sir, on behalf of all Members of this House, it is my honour and privilege to congratulate you on your election as Speaker. With your extensive experience as a Member of this House since 1984, and as a Deputy Speaker for 4 1/2 years from January 1989 to June 1993 and as a Minister thereafter, Members have no doubt that Parliamentary proceedings conducted in this Chamber, with you in the Chair, will continue to be in accordance with the best traditions of fairness, decorum and propriety. All of us, Members of this House, pledge to support you and cooperate with you in the discharge of your heavy responsibilities.

    Sir, on behalf of the Ministers and the Members, past and present during his tenure as Speaker, I would like to take this opportunity to pay tribute to Mr Tan Soo Khoon, your distinguished predecessor. [Applause.] Mr Tan had served as Deputy Speaker for nearly 4 years, from March 1985, before he was elected Speaker in the Seventh Parliament, in January 1989.

    In my congratulatory speech at that time, I said that Members had every confidence that Mr Tan would discharge his responsibilities with great distinction. He indeed lived up to our expectations and we were delighted to re-elect him as Speaker for two more terms in the 8th and 9th Parliaments.

    In 1989, on his election as Speaker, Mr Tan had said that "he hoped to maintain the high standards and traditions set by his predecessor (Dr Yeoh Ghim Seng)..

Column: 10

I found myself at times trying to unravel myself from some of the complexities of the rules of procedure." Mr Tan has amply fulfilled his pledge. He indeed upheld the dignity of this House by ensuring that order and decorum were observed. Although not legally trained, I believe he has managed to "unravel himself from some of the complexities of the rules of procedure".

    Under his charge as Speaker, our parliamentary debates and all other business of Parliament, including those of the Select Committees and the Committee of Privileges, proceeded most smoothly, efficiently and effectively. As some would put it, it is "like clock-work". He has guided the proceedings in this Chamber most fairly, always giving every opportunity for Members to speak. Despite some long rambling speeches made from time to time, Mr Tan was always patient in hearing them out. He was also indulgent on some Members who pressed on with repetitive questions even though the questions were already answered. He only interrupted when he thought that enough was enough.

    One of the key achievements during Mr Tan Soo Khoon's tenure as Speaker is the building of this new Parliament House which was officially opened on 4th October 1999. He chaired the Parliament Building Committee, formed in 1993, which planned and built this New Parliament House. He made many study visits to other parliaments and learned from them. As a result, this House has incorporated many upgraded features which will serve Parliamentarians well into the future.

    During his Speakership, Mr Tan also strengthened relations with and enhanced our status among ASEAN, Commonwealth and other Parliaments, through the various parliamentary delegations he led and conferences he attended.

    To bring Parliament closer to the people, Mr Tan initiated the Parliament Internet Homepage in May 1996. Internet-savvy readers, local and overseas, can now know more about our Parliament. In 1998, Mr Tan also set up an Education Department to educate Singaporeans about the
