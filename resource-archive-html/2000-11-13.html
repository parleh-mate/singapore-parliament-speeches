HAND, FOOT AND MOUTH DISEASE
(Update on outbreak)


    3. Mr Inderjit Singh asked the Acting Minister for the Environment if he will (i) give an update on the outbreak of the hand, foot and mouth disease; (ii) state whether the disease is under control; and (iii) inform what precaution his Ministry will take to avoid similar situation in the future.

    The Senior Minister of State for the Environment (Encik Sidek bin Saniff) (for the Acting Minister for the Environment): Mr Speaker, Sir, hand, foot and mouth disease (HFMD) is a common mild childhood disease. Very rarely does it result in deaths. However, on the morning of 30th September, two siblings aged one and two died, within two hours of each other. Upon notification that morning, the Commissioner of Public Health immediately activated the Task Force that was

formed two years ago following the outbreaks of HFMD in the region in 1998.

    The Task Force recommended the closure of all pre-schools effective from 2nd October. The objective was to isolate the children and protect them from contracting the disease. The Minister for the Environment accepted the recommendation. The directive went out immediately to all the pre-schools on the same day.

    The quick and decisive action by the Government, together with the concerted efforts of the public, pre-schools, media and employers proved to be effective. I am pleased to report to the House that the outbreak has been brought under control. The number of reported cases per day reached a peak of 258 on 4th October, and declined steadily to 9 on 31st October. All pre-schools have reopened since 16th October. The situation is now back to normal.

    On behalf of the multi-agency task force, I would like to take this opportunity to thank the parents and members of the public, pre-school and kindergarten teachers, doctors at the Kandang Kerbau Women and Children Hospital, the National University Hospital, the Singapore General Hospital and doctors in private practice, affected employers, and various Government agencies for their understanding, support and cooperation.

    HFMD is spread through close contact among children. The most effective measure to prevent future outbreaks is to maintain a high standard of hygiene in pre-school centres and at home. Parents and teachers also need to remain vigilant, and keep infected children away from other children. The multi-agency task force will continue to strengthen guidelines relating to hygiene standards and sick bay operations to prevent future outbreaks as much as we can.

TEENAGE ABORTIONS


    6. Mdm Claire Chiang See Ngoh asked the Minister for Health, in view of the recent demand from the community for parental consent in teenage abortions, whether he will (i) seek broader discussions and consensus on this matter by forming a review committee to reappraise the issues and (ii) adopt a fresh look in reviewing the Termination of Pregnancy Act to strengthen some level of monitoring on teenage abortions.

    Mr Lim Hng Kiang:

    My Ministry reviews the regulations and procedures in the Termination of Pregnancy Act regularly. We welcome feedback and suggestions on how to improve them. Many of the points raised in the recent debate on parental consent in teenage abortions are not new. If there are specific suggestions on how to improve the monitoring of teenage abortions, we are prepared to incorporate them.
