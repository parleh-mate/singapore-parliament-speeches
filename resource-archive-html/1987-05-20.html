DRUG ADDICTION
(Situation report)


    7. Dr Ho Tat Kin asked the Minister for Home Affairs what is the current situation in Singapore as regards drug addiction; has the number of cases been on the increase; and what measures will be taken to improve the situation.

     The Minister of State for Home Affairs (Dr Lee Boon Yang)(for the Minister for Home Affairs): Sir, I am replying on behalf of my Minister.

    Sir, the drug addiction problem in Singapore is under control. Over the last 10 years, we have brought the addict population down from 13,000 in 1977 to about 7,000. They are mainly heroin addicts.

    The infection rate has also been checked. In 1986, out of seven addicts arrested, only one was a new addict. The infection rate has been cut by more than half of the rate in 1977.

    The number of young drug offenders (below 20 years old) has also declined. In 1986, 185 or 5% of drug offenders were below 20 years of age whereas in the first year of Operation Ferret in 1977, 31.4% or 2,427 drug offenders arrested were below 20 years of age.

    While we have been effective in tackling the drug addiction problem as a whole, we are now faced with the residue problem of a growing number of hardcore addicts who have undergone repeated treatment and rehabilitation only to relapse after release from the DRC. At the end of 1986, there were 3,909 addicts in our DRCs. Of this number, 1,124 or 28.8% were hardcore addicts who were undergoing treatment and rehabilitation for the 4th time or more. A solution must be found for this

    Besides providing for retirement needs, the CPF Scheme also encourages home-ownership. Where loans are for the purchase of homes, both objectives are met. Somewhat different considerations apply to properties. The sums involved are much larger and the assets are relatively more difficult to buy and sell.

    On balance, the Government has not decided to allow CPF savings for refinancing of properties. However, if necessary, this issue could be reviewed at a later date.
