MRT LINE FROM BOON LAY MRT STATION
TO TUAS SECOND LINK

(Extension)


    1. Mr R. Ravindran asked the Minister for Communications whether there are plans to extend the MRT line from the Boon Lay MRT station to the Tuas Second Link Bridge (Customs/Immigration Checkpoint) and, if so, if he will indicate the date of completion of the extension line.
LIGHT RAIL TRANSIT SYSTEM LINKING
BOON LAY MRT STATION TO JURONG WEST


    2. Mr R. Ravindran asked the Minister for Communications whether there are plans to build a Light Rail Transit (LRT) system linking Boon Lay MRT station to the Jurong West Housing and Development Board estates encompassing Jurong West Neighbourhoods 6, 7, 8 and 9 and, if so, if he will indicate the date of completion of the LRT line.

    The Minister of State for Communications (Dr John Chen Seow Phun) (for the Minister for Communications): Sir, as Questions 1 and 2 are related, can I take them together?

    Mr Speaker: Yes.

    Dr John Chen Seow Phun: Sir, the Land Transport Authority (LTA) has assessed that an MRT line extension from Boon Lay Station to the Second Link in Tuas is not operationally viable at the moment. Neither will any benefit outweigh the high costs of the construction of such an extension. Nevertheless, LTA will continue to monitor the situation and review the feasibility of this line regularly as part of its long-term rail development plan. Under this plan, LTA has also been studying the feasibility of operating LRT systems in various parts of Singapore. The Jurong West housing estate is included in this study. Results of the study are expected to be ready in two years' time.

CHINESE AND JAPANESE GARDENS
(Free use by members of the public)


    3. Mr R. Ravindran asked the Minister for Trade and Industry whether his Ministry will consider opening up the Chinese and Japanese Gardens for free use by members of the public.

    The Senior Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for Trade and Industry (Mr Tang Guan Seng) (for the Minister for Trade and Industry): The Chinese and Japanese Gardens are managed as a business by Singapore Leisure Industries Pte Ltd (SLI), which is a subsidiary of JTC.

    SLI has invested $12.5 million in the Gardens and incurs about $1 million a year in operating expenses. Despite the admission charges of $4.50 per adult and $2 per child, the revenue is insufficient to defray the operating expenses and SLI has incurred a deficit of $240,000 in FY 97/98.

    To encourage the public to visit the Gardens more frequently, SLI introduced in August 1997 a scheme of unlimited entry, except during special functions, at a nominal rate of $20 per annum per person. SLI will continue to review the existing scheme and look into further measures to encourage public usage of the Gardens.
