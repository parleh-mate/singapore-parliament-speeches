REPORTS ON STEEL MILLS AND ON INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT


    2. Mr David Marshall asked the Minister for Finance whether it is the Government's intention to table (a) the Schereschewsky Report on Steel Mills and (b) the Winsemius Report on Industrial Development, and when will they be tabled.

    Dr Goh Keng Swee: Mr Speaker, Sir, it is not the intention of the Government to publish these two Reports. Both Reports were made to the Government on a confidential basis and cannot be published without the consent of the U.N, agencies to which the experts belong.

    The Schereschewsky Mission made recommendations on the establishment of a steel industry in Singapore which could be carried out in three phases. The first phase is to start immediately. The second phase is to be implemented when conditions are favourable and after certain technical studies have been completed.

    No time has been lost in carrying out the recommendations of the steel mission in respect of the first phase. The steel plant is in the course of establishment at Jurong.

    The Winsemius Mission made recommendations on the establishment of certain specific industries in various fields. In addition, they made recommendations on general economic policy as well as a review on present potential strength and sources and weaknesses.

    The expert missions sent by the United Nations are of value only if they are able to make recommendations to the best of their judgement and ability. They must not be inhibited by any possibility that their analysis of the problem and recommendations as to solutions would be the subject of public political

controversy. For this reason the Government cannot publish the Reports of the two expert missions.

    The technical details of the Winsemius Report, such as the type of industries that could be established, the scale of plant, manufacturing processes suitable to Singapore, and other technical details are being made available to investors who are contemplating setting up a factory in Singapore. This work is carried out by the Economic Development Board.

    Mr David Marshall: The Minister seems to have given two reasons. I do not know whether they are exclusive or supplementary.
