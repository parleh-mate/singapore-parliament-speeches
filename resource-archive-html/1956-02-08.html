Investigations in 1953 revealed a divorce rate of about 60 per cent of the corresponding marriage rate - a very disturbing figure. Muslim marriages and divorces are regulated by the Muslims Ordinance Chapter 57 of our Laws, and as the law stands at present, any one of the twelve Kathis in Singapore, as a Deputy Registrar of Muslim Marriages, can register a marriage or a divorce for any Muslim person who has been resident in Singapore for more than four months. Each of these Kathis also has the power of a civil court to enquire into and adjudicate upon claims for the payment of dowry, maintenance and such matters. These Kathis are appointed by the Governor, but they are not paid. They retain the fees for solemnising marriages and registering divorces, and appeals against their decisions go to the Registrar of Muslim Marriages who is not himself a Muslim.

some conflict of jurisdiction between the Kathis, and there is a tendency, perhaps not unnatural, for a Kathi to register a divorce rather than to attempt to effect a reconciliation between the parties; nor has there been adequate supervision of the Kathis by the Registrar of Muslim Marriages.
