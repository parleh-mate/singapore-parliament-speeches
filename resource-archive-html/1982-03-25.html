    1. Mr J.B. Jeyaretnam asked the Minister for Home Affairs whether he is aware that innocent persons found in food stalls in Housing and Development Board

estates in the afternoons of race days are being arrested and taken to Police stations and detained till the evening, and, if so, if he will explain why this is being done.

    The Minister of State for Law and Home Affairs (Prof. S. Jayakumar) (for the Minister for Home Affairs): Mr Speaker, Sir, I am answering this question on behalf of my Minister.

Column: 1609



    A serious problem which exists in HDB estates is that of illegal bookmaking and betting taking place during the afternoons of race days while races are in progress. Commonly known as "mini turf club activities", these have become blatant and widespread. On race days large groups of persons, sometimes as many as a hundred or more, congregate at various locations such as hawker centres, coffee shops, shophouses or void decks in HDB estates, to place bets on the results of horse races.

    In 1981 alone, the police received a total of 8,887 '999' calls complaining about such gambling activities. At least 2,400 of these calls or about 50 per race day referred directly to these mini turf club activities. In addition, the eight Police Land Divisions and the CID also received 405 such complaints. The undesirable activities of the mini turf clubs have also been widely discussed in the press, and I recall that last week Members also referred to this matter in this House. Secret society and other criminal activities are also associated with this illegal gambling.

    To deal with this problem which causes annoyance and apprehension to the residents in the HDB estates, the police conduct raids at locations where such activities take place. In 1981 alone, a total of 2,372 raids were conducted, resulting in the arrest of over 2,600 persons.

    Persons arrested during these raids are brought back to the police station together with whatever evidence that is seized during the raids. The arrested persons have to be questioned, statements have to be recorded and evidence has to be processed before a decision is taken to prosecute. When a large number of persons are arrested, this may take several hours. Those who cannot be prosecuted due to lack of evidence are released the same afternoon or evening.

    I can assure hon. Members that there has not been any indiscriminate arrest of persons by the police in these raids. Before every raid the police carry out careful surveillance to identify the culprits before moving in to arrest them. The police has to-date received no official complaint of any specific case of wrongful arrest.

HOUSING AND DEVELOPMENT BOARD
(Computerization)


    2. Dr Chau Sik Ting asked the Minister for National Development whether the Housing and Development Board has commenced its computerization programme, and, if so, what are its priority areas to improve efficiency through computerization, particularly in view of its shortage of manpower and the fact that it has the greatest degree of exposure to the public.

     The Minister of State for National Development (Mr Lee Yock Suan) (for the Minister for National Development): Mr Speaker, Sir, I am replying on behalf of my Minister. The HDB embarked on its computerization programme in the early 1970s. At that time computerization focussed on financial operations because of the big volume of accounts involved. The main areas of application included:

    (a) accounting system for rental and purchase instalment payments of HDB flats;

Column: 1612



    (b) direct transfer of CPF monthly instalments and other payments from CPF Board to HDB for purchase of HDB flats;

    (c) direct payment of instalments, rent and service charges through the GIRO system operated by POSB and private banks;

    (d) monthly and daily-rated employee payroll; and

    (e) office inventory control.

Computerisation has enabled the HDB to provide better and faster services.

    In the late 1970s, computerization was extended to cover other applications, such as data processing and statistical analysis of research studies involving large volumes of data for administration and planning purposes, and engineering and surveying projects.

    HDB recently purchased a special computer for drafting engineering and architectural drawings. This will relieve the shortage of professional and technical manpower by improving staff productivity.

    HDB plans to acquire a more advanced computer to provide on-line services in areas such as payment, enquiry, application and allocation of premises. It will also study ways to maximize usage of computers to obtain better building design, control and management.
