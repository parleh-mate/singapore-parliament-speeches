POWER STATION


    3. Dr Thio Chan Bee asked the Prime Minister whether he is aware that the new free-piston generating machinery installed in the St. James Power Station is no longer functioning properly, and what steps will be taken to avoid any danger of a sudden shortage of electricity leading to blackouts and rationing.

    The Prime Minister: Sir, it is not a question of the St. James Power Station no longer functioning properly because the new plant has not yet been put into commission.

    A tender for the construction of a Free Piston Gas Turbine Generating Plant at the St. James Power Station was accepted by the previous City Council in March 1957 to replace the obsolete and worn out steam plant which was then no longer serviceable. Construction and installation was completed towards the end of 1959, but the City Council has not - I repeat - has not yet taken over the plant as it has not passed the reliability test because of faulty materials and the technical modifications necessary. Defective parts are being replaced by the manufacturers

Column: 9

free of charge. The plant is expected to be available for full commission by September 1962.

    As regards the maintenance of electricity supplies, the Pasir Panjang Power Station Extension Scheme is now in progress and a seventh 25,000 K.W. turbo generator is scheduled for completion in June this year. Furthermore, a new power station to meet the needs of industrial expansion is also being built and a World Bank loan is being sought for this. The consultants for this project are already in Singapore.

    The first phase of the new station is for plant capable of generating 60 M.W. which is scheduled for commission in 1965. With these developments, it is not expected that there will be any sudden shortage of supply in the future. On the contrary, our total generating plant, once installed, should enable us to meet our expanding needs, including the Jurong Industrial Development project. However, I may add that the failure of the free-piston plant has been a great disappointment. The new plant was intended for peak loads and there have been anxious moments lately on this account during peak periods. It is possible that these may recur again between now and when the extension scheme in Pasir Panjang is ready in June this year. It is doubtful if the City Council should have agreed to install a free-piston plant. ABSENCE OF TEACHERS ON REOPENING OF SCHOOLS
(Request for statement)


    9. Mrs Seow Peck Leng asked the Minister for Education whether he is aware that when the schools reopened on 2nd January, 1962, some classes had no teachers and the children were told that a teacher would be available only after two weeks, and whether he will make a statement on the matter.

    Mr Yong Nyuk Lin: Mr Speaker, Sir, the recruitment of new teachers for the opening of schools each year is a massive annual event. The recruitment of more than 1,300 new teachers for the reopening of the new school year on 2nd January, 1962, was officially begun with advertisements published in the local newspapers calling for applicants on 26th October, 1961. A record number of 11,713 application forms were issued, and 6,542 completed forms were returned. Twenty-three selection boards were then constituted to interview 5,370 candidates. The process of calling up hundreds of those considered suitable, replacing those who have since decided not to accept appointment, getting them to sign the various requisite papers, having them medically examined, and issuing letters of appointment and posting orders, are time-consuming factors.

    As the Member for Mountbatten is fully aware, all this volume of work was done within two months. But for the devotion and loyalty of the staff of the Ministry and the long hours put in by the Establishment arid Inspectorate staff, principals of schools and lecturers of the Teachers' Training

Column: 19

Seventeen, now working in other Ministries, are waiting to be released from their present jobs, and another eight to be found who would be willing to be posted to island schools.

    To ensure that more time could be given to processing of future applicants and to complete full posting of new teachers to schools for the beginning of each school year, it is proposed to advertise in August this year for 1963 recruitment of teachers instead of waiting till October, i.e. two months earlier than was done last year.
