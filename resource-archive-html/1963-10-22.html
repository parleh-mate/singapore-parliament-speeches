Your election, Mr Speaker, is in keeping with our constitutional procedure and parliamentary practice prevailing in many countries. YANG DI-PERTUAN NEGARA'S SPEECH AT CEREMONIAL
OPENING OF ASSEMBLY
(Statement by the Prime Minister)


    The Prime Minister (Mr Lee Kuan Yew): Mr Speaker, Sir, I would like to explain briefly why it has not been possible to have the ceremonial opening of the Assembly with a speech from His Excellency the Yang di-Pertuan Negara at the first meeting following upon the general elections. On the 23rd July this year in the last Assembly, all the Opposition Parties - the Barisan Sosialis, the Alliance, and the United People's Party - and two Independents blocked a Bill for general election of 15 Members to the Central Parliament.

    Members are aware that the Speaker of Parliament has asked us for the names of the 15 representatives from Singapore. These Members will take their places as Members of the House of Representatives on 2nd November, in just over a week's time. It is not possible before the 2nd of November to have a speech by His Excellency the Yang di-Pertuan Negara, thereafter seven clear days' adjournment, followed by a debate on the motion of thanks to the Yang di-Pertuan Negara, and then a Bill for the election of 15 seats - all this to be completed before 2nd November. We have, therefore, had to make an adjustment and have, in today's business session, the swearing in of new Members and the passage of this Bill for 15 Members to the Central Parliament.

    After we have ascertained when the business sittings of Parliament in Kuala Lumpur will be in November and December, the date for the ceremonial opening of this Assembly will be fixed when His Excellency the Yang di-Pertuan Negara will give his speech setting out the programme and policy of the Government for the next year.
