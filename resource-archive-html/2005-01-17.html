    'That the following Address in reply to the Speech of the President be agreed to:-

    "We, the Parliament of the Republic of Singapore, express our thanks to the President for the Speech which he delivered on behalf of the Government at the Opening of the Second Session of this Parliament.".'.

reminder to all of us that life is full of uncertainties.  We cannot take for granted the peace and stability we enjoy today.  We must instead strive to preserve what we have achieved and at the same time not forgetting our duty to the rest of the world, despite our small size.

 

     Singapore is at a major turning point in history.  With the change in the Government leadership last year, we are now entering a new chapter.   The President outlined seven priority areas for the Government in his Address last week.  These are to:  secure Singapore's place in the world; to build a safe and secure environment; to build the economy; to invest in people; to augment our population and talent; to engage the youth; and to build a place for everyone.  These priority areas cover a broad spectrum of Government's policies.  They range from domestic issues to international relations, from bread and butter concerns to the more abstract issue of nation building.  However, there appears a common theme among them.  They all share the same objective, that of creating value for Singaporeans.

 

     First, we create value by securing regional and international recognition of Singapore through our active involvement in the international arena.  Our role in fostering international collaboration in the promotion of free trade, counter-terrorism measures and even the tsunami relief efforts, has elevated our international standing.  Many Singaporeans are proud that we can make positive contributions in a meaningful way.

 

     We also add value to Singapore by building a safe home, a prosperous economy and a vibrant community.  We want to make this place an attractive place to live, work and play.  We want to give each and every Singaporean a say in the nation building process so that we will have a strong sense of ownership and we will work together to create value for the

nation. Finally, we make sure that the value we have created can be enjoyed by every Singaporean, including those who may be less able. 

 

     The President's Speech is about creating value for Singaporeans.  The overall tone of the Speech was somewhat cautiously optimistic.  Optimistic, because the best is yet to be.  The future offers enormous opportunities as the region recovers from the Asian economic crisis and as the world becomes increasingly open.  With our resources, our people and our network, we are well placed to grasp these opportunities when they come.  Cautious, because the path ahead remains full of challenges.  Success will not come easy.  The world environment is changing rapidly.  We must move fast to adapt to these changes.  The process will not be without pain but the ultimate success is well worth it.

 

     Sir, I would like to share my views on four specific areas which I feel are crucial in preparing ourselves for the challenges ahead.  To me, these are the four pillars on which we create value for Singapore. 

 

     First, creating opportunities.  We have to offer more varieties and options in education, career, leisure and other areas.  In education, for example, we have seen many changes in the last two years.  The removal of the primary school streaming of EM1 and EM2 (and I still hope that the Minister will consider doing away with EM3 as well), the introduction of the Integrated Programme, the establishment of private schools, the changes to the Chinese Language programme, the proposed corporatisation of the two universities and the establishment of the fourth university.  These changes are aimed at creating more opportunities for Singaporeans to pursue their education here according to their interest and ability.

and contribute to the society within the limits of his ability, he must be considered successful, and we must help him to do so.
