ORAL ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS

DENGUE FEVER



    1. Mr Harun A. Ghani asked the Minister for the Environment how was the situation of dengue fever in 1998; how effective was the Aedes mosquito campaign and whether it was better than 1997's and, if not, what measures are being taken by his Ministry.

    The Minister for the Environment (Mr Yeo Cheow Tong): Sir, the number of dengue fever cases continued to rise to a record high of 5,258 in 1998. Almost all the cases were the milder dengue fever and not dengue haemorrhagic fever. There was also an increase in the Aedes mosquito house-index, which is a measure of the incidence of breedings detected in homes. The index rose from 1.03 in 1997 to 1.24 in 1998.

    A survey conducted by my Ministry showed that our public education programme on dengue has succeeded in alerting the public but it has not changed their habits generally. Many know that the Aedes mosquito transmits dengue fever

HAWKER STALLHOLDERS AND RESTAURANT OWNERS

(Suspension from businesses)



    2. Mr Harun A. Ghani asked the Minister for the Environment how many hawker stallholders and restaurant owners were suspended from their businesses in 1997 and 1998; and how many of them were repeat offenders.

    Mr Yeo Cheow Tong: Sir, in 1997, my Ministry suspended the licences of 10 hawker stallholders, 37 restaurant owners and 2 stallholders in coffeeshops. In 1998, the corresponding numbers were 22, 31 and 22 respectively. There was only one licensee, a hawker stallholder, who was suspended twice. The first suspension was for two weeks and the second for four weeks.
