    At present the Trade Unions Ordinance does not empower the Minister or the Registrar of Trade Unions to freeze the bank accounts of any trade union if information is received that the union

intends to utilise its funds for any purpose contrary to the provisions of the Ordinance or the rules of the union itself. While the Registrar has power to apply to the Court for an injunction to restrain any trade union from unlawful expenditure of its funds, this process in law, however, is not practicable especially when the matter has to be dealt with on an immediate basis. Because the present Ordinance does not provide the necessary powers, the Registrar of Trade Unions could only advise banks holding the accounts of unions to suspend payment of any cheques drawn on them. Although in the case of the three deregistered S.A.T.U. unions the banks co-operated with the Government, they were in a difficult position as the Government's request had no statutory backing. This being so, the banks would therefore be liable for damages if they were sued by their clients. As Government had perforce to freeze the funds of these unions, it was necessary, therefore, for the Government to give a categorical assurance that it would indemnify any legal costs arising from any proceedings as a result of their failing to honour the unions' cheques.

    Despite action taken by the Registrar to request the banks to withhold withdrawals which might be initiated by these three S.A.T.U. unions, two of the unions, however, were able to withdraw about $30,000 from their accounts in a number of banks before the banks could receive Government's indemnification assurance. The new section 15A provided in clause 2 of the Bill will now enable the Minister, where he is satisfied that the funds of any unions may be misused, to order any bank in Singapore not to pay any money or to cash any cheques of the unions for a period not exceeding three months. Non-compliance with this order will make the manager of the bank liable to punishment as set out in subsection (3) of section 15A. Subsection (2) will, of course, absolve the bank of any liability arising from compliance with the order
