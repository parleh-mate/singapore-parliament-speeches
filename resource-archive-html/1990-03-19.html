ORAL ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS

MORE LEISURE AND RECREATIONAL FACILITIES
(Development)


    1. Mr Chew Heng Ching asked the Minister for National Development if his Ministry will step up efforts to develop more leisure and recreational facilities, particularly in our offshore islands.

     The Senior Minister of State for National Development (Dr Lee Boon Yang) (for the Minister for National Development): Mr Speaker, Sir, my Ministry recognises that, with rising affluence and higher aspirations, Singaporeans will demand more leisure facilities. For the long term, we have safeguarded sufficient land for recreational use for a population of up to 4 million.

    In the immediate future, the Parks and Recreation Department will develop new parks and improve existing parks at an estimated cost of $156 million from 1989 to 1993. The parks will have diverse themes and new facilities. For example, adventure parks and nature parks will be developed. Some of the parks will contain facilities for skate-boarding, BMX (bike) trails and traditional games.

    Waterfront land will also be better used for recreation, water sports and marinas. The Urban Redevelopment Authority is

Column: 414

now studying how the Tanjong Rhu and the Changi Point areas can be developed for such purposes.

    Apart from new developments, natural areas of high environmental quality have been safeguarded for the enjoyment of the public. For example, we will preserve the mangrove park in Pasir Ris and the bird sanctuary in Sungei Buloh.

    The cluster of southern islands, namely, St John's, Lazarus and Kusu Islands are also suitable for recreational use. The Singapore Tourist Promotion Board has engaged consultants to study how the recreational potential of these islands can be realised. Some of the preliminary ideas include the linking of these islands and the creation of areas for swimming, boating as well as facilities for marinas.

     Mr Chew Heng Ching (Eunos GRC): Sir, would the Senior Minister of State give us an indication of the time-frame for the development of all these various facilities? Are we talking of one year, two years, three years or five years?

     Dr Lee Boon Yang: Mr Speaker, Sir, the feasibility study is just being carried out . We will get a better idea of the time-frame of these developments after the study is available and we can have a chance to look at the recommendations. Some of the proposed land use on the offshore islands will entail additional reclamation and it could take quite a few years to do such a reclamation.

MEDICAL SUBSIDIES IN TERTIARY INSTITUTIONS
(Reduction)


    2. Mr Yeo Toon Chia asked the Minister for Education what are the reasons for the wide variation in medical subsidy per student in the four tertiary institutions which ranges from $9 per student in the Ngee Ann Polytechnic to $64 per student in the Nanyang Technological Institute; and what measures are being taken to reduce the higher medical subsidies for the Singapore Polytechnic, National University of Singapore and the Nanyang Technological Institute.

     The Senior Minister of State for Education (Dr Tay Eng Soon) (for the Minister for Education): Mr Speaker, Sir, I am replying on behalf of my Minister.

    Sir, the Auditor-General has, in his latest Report, drawn the attention of the Ministry to the wide variations in the provision of subsidised medical services to students at our tertiary institutions. In FY 87, the extent of subsidy per student ranged from $9 per student in the Ngee Ann Polytechnic to $64 per student in the Nanyang Technological Institute. The variations in the subsidies are due to the different arrangements made by the respective tertiary institutions to provide medical services to their students. Generally, the subsidies are higher in those institutions which employ full-time doctors and nurses to man their clinics. The Ministry is conducting a review on the provision of medical services to students in our tertiary institutions and the level of subsidy per student.

APPLICATIONS BY WORK PERMIT HOLDERS TO CONTRACT MARRIAGE WITH SINGAPOREANS
(Figures)


    3. Mr Chew Heng Ching asked the Minister for Labour how many male and female work permit holders applied to contract marriage with Singaporeans in 1989, and how many applications have been approved.

     The Minister for Labour (Mr Lee Yock Suan): Mr Speaker, Sir, in 1989, a total of 2,261 work permit holders comprising 466 males and 1,795 females applied to contract marriage with Singaporeans. A total of 1,441 applications were granted approval in 1989, comprising applications by 311 male and 1,130 female work permit holders. This total is equivalent to about two-thirds of the applications received during the year.

     Mr Chew Heng Ching: Sir, would the Minister enlighten the House on the criteria for granting approval to these applications?

     Mr Lee Yock Suan: Mr Speaker, Sir, we look mainly at the qualifications of the work permit holder, ie, whether he or she has skills or qualifications which are of use to Singapore. We also look at the total situation, eg, whether the intending spouse in Singapore is able to support the work permit holder.

    As explained in Parliament last year, we have also adjusted the criteria to be consistent with the relaxation in the immigration policy for Hong Kong people. So long as the Singaporean spouses meet the same criteria we approve them on the same basis as the Hong Kong people.
