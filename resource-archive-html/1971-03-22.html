ORAL ANSWER TO QUESTION
MALAYSIA-SINGAPORE AIRLINES
(Restructure of)


    1. Mr Hwang Soo Jin asked the Minister for Finance whether it was not possible to reconcile the conflicting objectives of the Singapore and Malaysian Governments on the running of MSA, chiefly in relation to the expansion of Malaysian domestic air services, in order to avoid a dissolution of their partnership. Next, what is the current state of the negotiations and what are the objectives of the proposed Singapore national airline.

    The Minister for Finance (Mr Hon Sui Sen): Mr Speaker, Sir, it has become common knowledge that the policies and objectives of the Governments of Singapore and Malaysia towards Malaysia-Singapore Airlines have become divergent. Differences have arisen from time to time in recent years.

    On the 2nd September, 1970, the Malaysian Ministry of Foreign Affairs delivered a note to the Singapore High Commission in Kuala Lumpur requesting the Singapore Government to designate a committee of officials to discuss with their committee the creation of a

Column: 976

Malaysian Domestic Airline. On the 13th November, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs delivered a note in reply to the Malaysian High Commission in Singapore nominating this committee.

    The differences of objectives between the two Governments have become irreconcilable. A parting of the ways was unavoidable. Continuation of the partnership would only have led to constant friction and unnecessary conflict.

    The Singapore Government's interest is centred upon a fast-expanding commercially viable operation with flights on international routes to Europe and the United States. The Malaysian Government has different priorities. A major point of disagreement was the proposal to expand MSA domestic air services regardless of the loss forecast by management.

    Now that the decision has been taken to dissolve the co-ownership and joint control of MSA, it is in the best interests of both Governments that the proposed restructuring of the company should be proceeded with as expeditiously as possible. There are good reasons for proceeding with deliberate speed. First, in the airline business nothing stands

Column: 977

still. The transitional period before restructuring is completed is not conducive to the taking of important decisions on the expansion of services and the acquisition of new aircraft. Secondly, staff of the company, contractors, agents, suppliers and clients would find a long transitional period unsettling not knowing where exactly they stand. Thirdly, Government officials as well as the staff of MSA who are directing, planning and undertaking the arrangements for the restructuring of the company cannot have their attention distracted from their normal duties for a prolonged period of uncertainty and restlessness pending the final hiving off into two separate airlines.

    Negotiations between officials of the two Governments have resulted in a measure of agreement. There are a few points on which further talks are to take place. It has been agreed that, first, the restructuring will be completed by the end of 1972 or any earlier date that may be agreed upon by the two Governments; secondly, that the Fokker aircraft will be transferred to the proposed Malaysian national airline and the Boeing 707 aircraft to the Singapore national airline; thirdly, that projects approved will be implemented and that projects required for the orderly growth and expansion of the company will not be held up on account of the proposed restructuring of the airline. The Governments have also appointed, through MSA, a reputable firm of accountants to value the assets, both tangible and intangible, of the company; the report of the accountants is to be submitted by 30th June this year.

    The Singapore Government has proposed that, to facilitate the establishment of the Malaysian national airline, the internal operations of MSA should over a period of time be separated, and placed under the control of a "Malaysian Division" of the company. In the near future, such a Division would become the nucleus of the Malaysian national airline and would be handed over as such. It is the intention of the Singapore Government that this transfer should take place as soon as possible.
