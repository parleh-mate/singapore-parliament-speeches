PUBLIC AND PRIVATE SECTOR APARTMENTS

(Replacement of window rivets)

     2.   Dr Amy Khor Lean Suan asked the Minister for National Development (a) whether he can provide an update on the latest known number of public and private sector apartments that are required to, but have yet to get the rivets of their windows replaced; (b) what measures the Ministry has taken or will take to ensure that the owners of these apartments comply with the requirement by the deadline set; and (c) what are the implications for flat owners if they are unable to comply by the deadline.

     3.  Dr Lily Neo asked the Minister for National Development if he will consider postponing the 30th September 2005 cut-off date for retrofitting windows in view of the fact that many owners of HDB and private apartments may have difficulty in meeting this deadline.

 

 

     The Minister for National Development (Mr Mah Bow Tan):  Mr Deputy Speaker, Sir, may I have your permission to take Question Nos. 2 and 3 together as they are related?

     Mr Deputy Speaker:   Yes.

 

     Mr Mah Bow Tan:  Sir, first of all, let me explain why we carried out the window retrofitting exercise.  Last year, the incidence of falling windows rose sharply, from 107 cases in 2003 to 133 cases in 2004.  They were mainly caused by corroded aluminium rivets in casement windows.  In a high-rise built-up environment like ours, falling windows pose a serious danger to the life and limb of our residents.  To address this, we required all casement windows fitted with aluminium rivets to be replaced with stainless steel rivets.  In view of the large number of windows involved, we gave a one-year grace period.

     In the last one year, BCA and HDB embarked on a major campaign to raise public awareness on the need to retrofit their windows.  This was done through direct mail to HDB residents and to management corporations of private estates, press releases, newspaper and radio advertorials, constituency exhibitions and other forums. BCA and HDB trained additional window contractors and linked them up with home-owners.  Town Councils and Management Corporations were also roped in to help coordinate the window retrofitting exercise in their estates.

 

     The response from residents has been encouraging.  To date, the vast majority, or about 94%, of households required to retrofit their windows have already done so.  Dr Lily Neo asked whether the deadline for retrofitting would be extended when it expires on 30th September this year.  My Ministry will not be extending the deadline.  It is not in the interest of public safety to do so.  The grace period of one year has been more than adequate.  The vast majority has had no problem complying.  The small minority who has not yet done so will be given additional help to do so.  Dr Amy Khor asked for a breakdown of these residents.  To-date, about 12,000 units have not yet fixed their windows. Of these, 4,000 are HDB units and 8,000 are private units.  They constitute about 3% of 150,000 HDB flats with casement windows that need to be retrofitted and about 20% of private flats.

 

     Dr Khor also asked what additional measures would be taken to help those who have not retrofitted their windows.  HDB and BCA have increased the number of hotlines that home-owners can call to seek assistance, and deployed more staff to tend to queries.  They have also appointed more window contractors to meet the increase in demand.  Home-owners who need assistance can call the HDB or BCA hotline for help.

Errant contractors who are found to be involved in malpractices like over-charging, providing misleading information or rendering poor service, would be fined or debarred from HDB contracting works.  So far, HDB has taken action against 49 contractors.

 

     Dr Khor asked about the implications for flat owners who are unable to retrofit their window rivets by the deadline.  With the stepped up efforts by HDB and BCA, I expect that virtually all home-owners will be able to meet the deadline.  Those who still have problems retrofitting their windows should contact HDB or BCA for advice and assistance immediately.  After the deadline, BCA will take action under the window retrofitting order against home-owners who do not have valid reasons for not fixing their affected windows.

 

     May I remind all flat-owners that this exercise is to save lives. With the cooperation of everyone, I hope we will be able to make our housing estates safe for our families, our friends and ourselves.
