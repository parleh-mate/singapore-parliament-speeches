    3. Dr Wong Kwei Cheong asked the Minister for the Environment what measures the Government has taken to minimise the shrinking of the ozone layer.

     The Minister for the Environment (Dr Ahmad Mattar): Mr Speaker, Sir, the depletion of the ozone layer has been linked mainly to the presence of chlorofluorocarbons, more commonly referred to as CFCs, and halons in the stratosphere. It is a problem of global dimensions and only through concerted international co-operation can we hope to prevent further damage to the ozone layer.

Column: 910

use of some types of CFCs that are very damaging to the ozone layer. Singapore became party to this treaty in January 1989.

    Further reductions will be made in the quantity of controlled CFCs imported into and used in Singapore to 80% of 1986 levels commencing July 1993 and 50% of 1986 levels commencing July 1998. Three types of halons, controlled under the Montreal Protocol, will be frozen at 1986 levels commencing January 1992.
