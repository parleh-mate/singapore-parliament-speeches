    On this point, if Members would bear with me, permit me now to tell you a story. This is about Calvin Coolidge who became President of the United States of America when his predecessor died in office in 1923. His political slogan was "Keeping cool with Coolidge", which stood for, amongst other things, the fact that he was not easily ruffled. Coolidge was often brief and to the point, in fact, so brief that he was noted for his closed-mouth and was nicknamed `Silent Cal'. Well, the story goes like this. A reporter once asked him, "Mr President, what do you think about Prohibition?" "No comment", replied Coolidge. "Will you say something about unemployment?", asked the reporter again. "No", came the reply. "Will you give us your views of the world situation?", persuaded the exasperated reporter. "No", was the reply from the President. Just as the disappointed reporter started to leave, Coolidge said, "Wait". Hopefully the man turned around with earnest anticipation, only to hear Coolidge remind him, "Please don't quote me.".

    Coolidge's disinclination to speak did not hurt him. In fact, so popular was he that in 1924 Coolidge was elected to another term in one of the biggest landslides in US Presidential elections history.

    The moral of the story is this. We need not have the taciturnity of `Silent Cal' but you see, Honourable Members, brevity does have its virtues.
