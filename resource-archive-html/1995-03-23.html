    I shall now elaborate on each of these amendments.

The Full Commutation of Pensions

    Pensions will be fully commuted in the following manner. First, it shall be assumed that pensions will be paid for a period of 26 years, which is the average of life expectancies at the age 50 (28.2 years) and age 55 (23.9 years). The pensions stream will then be fully commuted by taking its net present value, using a discount rate of 5% per annum. Using this formula, $1 of monthly pensions paid for 26 years will be equivalent to $175.14 of gratuity. Clause 4 of the Bill provides for this full commutation factor.

The Death Gratuity Formula

    The new formula for computing death gratuity will be such that if the officer dies after attaining the minimum age to draw pension, then his death gratuity will be equal to the full amount of the fully commuted pension. In other words, you will lose nothing if you die after the age when you could have started drawing pension. If he dies before the pension-drawing age, his gratuity will be the fully commuted pension, but discounted by the number of years to go from the age of death to the minimum age for drawing pensions, subject to a minimum death gratuity of one year's pensionable emoluments. Clause 4 of the Bill provides for this revised formula for computing death gratuities, which will be more advantageous to the estates of the officers.

Column: 1075



The Anomaly in Death Gratuity Provisions

    Currently, when an officer is retired early, his pension benefits will be suspended until he reached the minimum pensions drawing age. If he dies within one year of retiring without drawing his pension, a death gratuity of one year's pensionable emoluments would be paid to his estate. For instance, if an officer retires at 45 with his pensions suspended, and he dies before reaching 46, then his estate will receive a death gratuity. But if he dies after 46, but before 50, when he could start drawing pensions, his estate will not get a death gratuity. This is an anomaly. Clause 4 of the Bill will rectify this, by providing for a death gratuity which is equal to the fully commuted pension, but discounted by the number of years between the minimum pension-drawing age and the age of death, subject to a minimum death gratuity of one year's pensionable emoluments.

Clause 3 of the Bill will correct this.
