Announcement by the Chairman


    The Chairman: Before we resume I would like to clear a point under the Standing Orders which was raised yesterday by the Honourable Member for Tanjong Pagar and commented upon by myself.

    The item under discussion was:

    "Sub-head 1, new item - Permanent Secretary (Establishment), Grade C ($1,950)... $23,400.00"

under Head 8, Chief Secretary. The Honourable Member stated that the reason why he had not tabled any amendment to this item was because it would have required the Governor's consent, and it was doubtful if the Governor would allow Members of the Opposition to interfere with the terms and conditions of service of serving officers.

    I commented that there was nothing to prevent an honourable Member from giving notice of a motion to reduce any item in any head of expenditure, that he might be faced with having to obtain the Governor's recommendation or consent, but that I did not think that he was entitled to assume that he would not get the recommendation or consent of the Governor.

    I have had the opportunity to look into the matter a little more closely and I am satisfied that, in this particular case, it was incorrect to presuppose that it was in fact necessary that the Governor's recommendation or consent would have had to be signified. The relevant portion of paragraph (1) (b) of Standing Order 33 reads:

    "Except with the recommendation or consent of the Governor signified thereto, the

Column: 1272

Assembly shall not proceed upon any bill or motion, which, in the opinion of the Speaker or the Attorney-General, would effect any alteration in the salary, allowances or conditions of service (including leave, passages and promotion) of any public officer...".

    As I see it, the provision under the relevant item is for a new post of Permanent Secretary (Establishment) and no public officer has as yet been appointed to that post. In my view, therefore, no public officer is affected and consequently the Standing Order would not have applied. It follows that the Honourable Member in fact would not have required the Governor's consent had he given notice of an amendment to reduce the sum allotted to that particular item. I thought it was wise that I should correct that misunderstanding.
