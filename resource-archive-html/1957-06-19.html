    1. Mr Lim Choon Mong asked the Chief Minister whether he will make a statement regarding the transfer of Christmas Island to Australia.

Column: 2024

the south of Singapore. This island does not belong to Singapore. The Singapore Government was notified of the decision of the United Kingdom Government to effect the transfer, and accordingly noted the position.

(Enquiry)


    12. Mr John Ede asked the Minister for Local Government, Lands and Housing-

    (a) how many tenants of S.I.T. shop premises in the Alexandra / Queenstown area from Redhill Road to Strathmore Avenue are being required to pay increases in rent from 1st June, 1957, and what is

Column: 2041

the average percentage increase in rent for these premises;

    (b) why these increases are being demanded after approximately one year's tenancy only; and

    (c) whether he will cause an enquiry to be made into the justification for these increases, and whether, in the meantime, he will give an assurance that these increases will be postponed.

    Inche Abdul Hamid: Mr Speaker, Sir, I am informed by the Singapore Improvement Trust as follows:

    (a) The number of terrace and shop premises in the Alexandra/Queenstown area which were required to pay an increase in rent was 39 out of the total number of 66 rentals reviewed by the Board of Trustees of the Singapore Improvement Trust. Of the remaining 27 rentals, there were 25 where the rents remained unaltered, and two cases of a reduction of $10 and $25 per month respectively. The average increase in rent was $25, which is equal to a percentage increase in rent of 16.7 per cent. The original rents were based by tender.

    (b) It was previously the policy of the Board of Trustees to review all new shop rentals after a minimum of twelve months' tenancy. On first letting, the true market value cannot be correctly assessed, and is therefore only an estimate. After a shopping centre has been established for at least twelve months, a clearer picture can be obtained, and an adjustment is then made either by decreasing or increasing the rentals, having regard to the past experience of the letting demand and the established market value. However, Sir, the Board at its last meeting decided that such a review should be made only after three years.

    (c) The Board has discretion to fix rents for the tenancies of shop premises and it is therefore a question for decision by the Board, but, as I have already said, the Board has now decided to review rents only after a

Column: 2042

    Mr John Ede: Mr Speaker, Sir, am I to understand from the Minister's statement that the increases will not now be enforced?

    Inche Abdul Hamid: Sir, the increases will not now be enforced, but the decreases will be enforced.

HAWKER PROBLEMS


    1. Mr Lim Cher Kheng asked the Minister for Local Government, Lands and Housing whether he will appoint a Committee to examine the hawker problems and make recommendations thereon.

    Inche Abdul Hamid Bin Haji Jumat: Government has decided that it would be desirable in principle to set up a Hawkers' Advisory Committee consisting of the following members : -

    (a) two nominees of the Government;

    (b) two nominees of the City Council;

    (c) two representatives of hawkers to be chosen in consultation with the appropriate hawkers associations; and

    (d) an independent Chairman, who may be a housewife.

    The Committee would advise the appropriate authorities on problems relating to hawkers, generally or specifically.

    The City Council is intimately concerned with the hawkers problem, and its views and co-operation are therefore being sought before the Committee is formally constituted.

    It is not intended that this Committee should usurp the functions of the City Council in its handling of the hawker problem, but as this problem has assumed major proportions recently, Government is of the opinion that a purely advisory body representing all parties concerned in this matter should now be established.
