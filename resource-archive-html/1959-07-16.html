There is a Parliamentary Under-Secretary when the Minister is a Minister of State and a Parliamentary Secretary when the Minister is not a Minister of State, and there is provision in that Act for the appointment of 27 such Parliamentary Secretaries.

Under the Ceylon Order in Council, there is provision for the appointment of Parliamentary Secretaries, and such appointments cannot exceed the number of Ministers who are appointed. You will see, Mr Speaker and Members of the House, that our Bill provides that the Parliamentary Secretary will be appointed by the Prime Minister or by a Minister, and will perform such functions as may be specified in his letter of appointment.     The main purpose of this Bill is to make temporary provisions to suspend for a period of six months those sections of the Control of Rent Ordinance which operate to the detriment of legal farmers and other legal tenants.

    Mr Speaker, with the present law as it is, we have found that many unscrupulous building societies or landlords have acquired acres and acres of agricultural land at a very low nominal price. There are hundreds of families of farmers who have paid their rents sometimes for two or three generations. These building societies, or in some eases unscrupulous persons, buy over the land at an average of a few hundred dollars an acre, and get the S.I.T. and the City Council to approve their building plans. So long as the plans are in order, the S.I.T. and the City Council will have to pass them. These unscrupulous contractors will take such building plans, engage lawyers, go to the court and seek the eviction of the farmers who, in certain cases, to my personal knowledge, have been staying on the land as legal tenants for three generations.

    In the course of eviction, no compensation is ever given. Neither is there the humane consideration to provide alternative sites. When the Government evicts people from the land in order to develop the land for the benefit of the nation and the public at large, compensation is normally given, and more often suitable alternative sites are offered. But this is not the case with these unscrupulous societies and other persons.

    Now, Mr Speaker, it is the intention of Government to introduce at an early date new legislation to assist farmers and householders, but definitely not illegal squatters of land. The idea of the new legislation is to assist legal farmers
